# APPENDIX I — BILL OF RIGHTS
## CONDITIONAL CIVIL GUARANTEES

DOCUMENT: BILL OF RIGHTS  
AUTHORITY: BULF-CONSTITUTION APPENDIX I  
STATUS: ACTIVE  
RATIFIED: 2026-05-23

---

# PREAMBLE

The Coalition recognizes that stable governance requires not only enforcement authority, but clearly defined civic guarantees to ensure orderly societal function.

The following rights are therefore recognized and protected insofar as their exercise does not threaten:

- Coalition continuity
- Public order
- Infrastructure stability
- Economic function
- Classified operations
- Strategic security

No right recognized herein shall supersede emergency continuity authority.

---

# ARTICLE I — RIGHT TO EXISTENCE

Registered citizens and authorized residents possess the right to continued habitation within Coalition territory provided they remain compliant with Coalition law.

This right may be suspended under:

- Treason investigations
- Tribunal rulings
- Quarantine orders
- Emergency relocation directives
- Continuity emergencies

---

# ARTICLE II — RIGHT TO SHELTER

Citizens possess the right to access approved residential housing zones where available.

Unauthorized settlements, underground habitation, or unregistered structures are not protected.

Housing assignments remain subject to district authority.

---

# ARTICLE III — RIGHT TO FOOD AND WATER

The Coalition recognizes access to food and water as essential to continuity.

Ration systems shall therefore operate during periods of scarcity.

Distribution priority may be adjusted according to:

- Labor classification
- Security role
- Emergency conditions
- Population density
- Strategic necessity

---

# ARTICLE IV — RIGHT TO EMPLOYMENT

Citizens possess the right to labor assignment and economic participation through approved Coalition systems.

The Coalition may redirect labor toward continuity-critical sectors where necessary.

Refusal of emergency labor assignment is not protected.

---

# ARTICLE V — RIGHT TO MEDICAL ACCESS

Citizens may access approved medical treatment through authorized providers.

Unregistered medical treatment, experimental procedures, and unauthorized pharmaceuticals remain prohibited.

Public health directives supersede individual preference during emergencies.

---

# ARTICLE VI — RIGHT TO MOVEMENT

Citizens may travel within authorized districts subject to:

- Curfew restrictions
- Checkpoint procedures
- Security zoning
- Registration verification
- Infrastructure exclusions

Movement into restricted areas is not protected.

---

# ARTICLE VII — RIGHT TO SPEECH

Citizens may express opinions regarding daily civic matters provided such expression does not:

- Undermine continuity
- Encourage unrest
- Spread destabilizing information
- Reveal classified operations
- Promote hostile ideologies
- Interfere with security operations

The Coalition recognizes that unrestricted speech during instability may threaten public safety.

---

# ARTICLE VIII — RIGHT TO ASSEMBLY

Citizens may gather peacefully where gatherings:

- Remain registered
- Avoid obstruction
- Do not challenge Coalition legitimacy
- Do not interfere with operations
- Do not exceed emergency restrictions

Assemblies may be dispersed where continuity concerns arise.

---

# ARTICLE IX — RIGHT TO PROPERTY

Citizens may own personal property subject to Coalition regulation.

The Coalition reserves authority to:

- Inspect
- Seize
- Requisition
- Restrict
- Demolish
- Redistribute

property where continuity requires.

Ownership of strategic materials is not guaranteed.

---

# ARTICLE X — RIGHT TO DUE PROCESS

Citizens accused of offenses possess the right to review before authorized judicial or tribunal authority.

Certain proceedings may remain classified.

Emergency continuity powers may temporarily suspend standard procedural protections.

---

# ARTICLE XI — RIGHT TO EDUCATION

Citizens possess the right to Coalition-approved education.

Educational systems shall prioritize:

- Infrastructure literacy
- Civic obedience
- Industrial competence
- Historical continuity
- Strategic awareness

Unauthorized educational material is not protected.

---

# ARTICLE XII — RIGHT TO SECURITY

The Coalition shall maintain BWARS and NSIC systems to protect citizens from:

- External threats
- Rogue factions
- Criminal instability
- Infrastructure sabotage
- Economic collapse
- Strategic attack

Security measures may require surveillance and movement regulation.

---

# ARTICLE XIII — RIGHT TO PETITION

Citizens may submit grievances, requests, appeals, and administrative concerns through authorized channels.

Petitions encouraging destabilization, separatism, or anti-Coalition activity are prohibited.

---

# ARTICLE XIV — RIGHT TO INFORMATION

Citizens may access public information approved for release by Coalition authorities.

Classified information is exempt from disclosure.

Truth alone does not guarantee public release authority.

---

# ARTICLE XV — RIGHT TO SURVIVAL DURING EMERGENCY

During disasters, attacks, shortages, or continuity crises, the Coalition shall attempt to preserve:

- Population stability
- Infrastructure operation
- Food distribution
- Energy production
- Medical continuity
- Governmental function

Emergency powers may override ordinary rights during such periods.

---

# ARTICLE XVI — LIMITATIONS CLAUSE

All rights recognized herein exist conditionally upon:

- Compliance with Coalition law
- Loyalty to continuity structures
- Preservation of public order
- Strategic necessity

Rights may be suspended, limited, or reinterpreted during periods of instability.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Citizens are reminded:

Rights exist because order exists.  
Order exists because the Coalition remains capable of enforcing it.

---

END OF APPENDIX
