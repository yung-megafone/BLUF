# SECTION I — PURPOSE

The following statutes define acts classified as High Treason against the BluCor Strategic Coalition, its governing structures, strategic continuity systems, authorized intelligence branches, and protected operational interests.

High Treason is recognized as the most severe category of offense under Coalition law.

Acts designated under these statutes are considered existential threats to territorial stability, governmental continuity, and public order.

---

# SECTION II — DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this document:

"Coalition Authority" shall refer to:
- BluCor governing structures
- BWARS command authority
- NSIC operational divisions
- Authorized WXBRO personnel
- Recognized High Table leadership
- Strategic continuity entities

"Hostile Action" shall include both direct and indirect activity intended to weaken, expose, destabilize, obstruct, infiltrate, or undermine Coalition operations.

Intent is not required where damages to Coalition stability are determined to have occurred.

---

# SECTION III — ACTS OF HIGH TREASON

The following offenses shall constitute High Treason.

---

## §3.1 Unauthorized Disclosure

Any individual who knowingly discloses classified information regarding:

- Coalition infrastructure
- NSIC operations
- Strategic technologies
- BWARS deployments
- Restricted facilities
- Orbital systems
- Surveillance programs
- Continuity directives

without proper authorization commits High Treason.

---

## §3.2 Collaboration With Hostile Entities

Providing assistance, intelligence, materials, transportation, funding, or logistical support to:

- Dissident organizations
- Rogue factions
- Sanctioned entities
- Unauthorized militias
- External hostile actors

shall constitute High Treason.

---

## §3.3 Strategic Sabotage

Intentional interference with:

- Power systems
- Communications infrastructure
- Transportation systems
- Treasury reserves
- Surveillance architecture
- Weapons platforms
- Coalition archives

shall be classified as High Treason where operational disruption exceeds acceptable thresholds.

---

## §3.4 Unauthorized Weapons Development

The unauthorized creation, possession, testing, transfer, or concealment of:

- Strategic weapons
- Nuclear-capable systems
- Experimental payloads
- Restricted research materials
- Coalition-classified technologies

constitutes High Treason regardless of operational intent.

---

## §3.5 Historical Falsification

The dissemination of narratives determined to:

- Contradict official Coalition findings
- Undermine public trust
- Challenge continuity legitimacy
- Expose compartmentalized operations
- Reframe sanctioned events

may constitute High Treason under NSIC review authority.

---

## §3.6 Infiltration and Espionage

Unauthorized acquisition of restricted information through:

- Surveillance
- Impersonation
- Unauthorized access
- Data extraction
- Covert observation
- Communications interception

shall constitute High Treason.

---

## §3.7 Failure of Reporting Duty

Personnel possessing knowledge of treasonous activity who fail to report such activity through authorized channels may themselves be charged under accessory provisions of High Treason statutes.

---

# SECTION IV — ENFORCEMENT AUTHORITY

The following entities possess enforcement jurisdiction under these statutes:

- BWARS Internal Security
- NSIC Investigative Divisions
- Coalition Tribunal Authorities
- Authorized WXBRO Enforcement Units

NSIC Omega retains emergency superseding authority where continuity threats are identified.

---

# SECTION V — CONTAINMENT PROTOCOLS

Where High Treason is suspected, Coalition authorities may authorize:

- Detainment without public disclosure
- Communications restrictions
- Asset seizure
- Relocation to restricted facilities
- Information suppression
- Operational containment actions

Additional emergency measures may be authorized under classified continuity directives.

---

# SECTION VI — SENTENCING

Penalties for High Treason may include:

- Permanent imprisonment
- Indefinite containment
- Asset forfeiture
- Citizenship revocation
- Historical record removal
- Execution under tribunal authority

Sentencing determination shall consider:

- Strategic damages caused
- Information exposure severity
- Threat continuation risk
- Political destabilization potential

---

# SECTION VII — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Coalition stability depends upon centralized continuity, informational control, and coordinated authority.

Threats against the Coalition are recognized not merely as crimes against government, but as attacks against societal order itself.

The preservation of order supersedes the preservation of individual privilege.
