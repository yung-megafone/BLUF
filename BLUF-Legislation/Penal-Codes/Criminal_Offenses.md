# SECTION I — PURPOSE

The Criminal Offenses statutes establish the general criminal code of the BluCor Strategic Coalition.

These statutes define unlawful conduct affecting public order, institutional stability, civil compliance, property integrity, labor discipline, controlled movement, approved speech, morality, public health, and security continuity.

The Coalition recognizes liberty only where liberty does not interfere with order.

---

# SECTION II — GENERAL AUTHORITY

Coalition criminal authority may be enforced by:

- BWARS Civil Security
- BWARS Internal Security
- NSIC Investigative Divisions
- Treasury Enforcement Officers
- Tribunal Officers
- Authorized WXBRO personnel
- Facility commanders
- District administrators

Emergency directives may expand enforcement power without prior public notice.

---

# SECTION III — OFFENSE CLASSIFICATIONS

Criminal offenses shall be classified as follows:

- **Class C Offense** — Minor disorder or administrative noncompliance
- **Class B Offense** — Public order violation or repeat misconduct
- **Class A Offense** — Serious criminal violation
- **Security Offense** — Conduct affecting Coalition safety or infrastructure
- **Continuity Offense** — Conduct affecting governmental stability
- **Tribunal Offense** — Conduct eligible for military tribunal proceedings
- **Capital Offense** — Conduct punishable by permanent containment or execution

Classification may be elevated where conduct occurs near protected infrastructure, involves organized activity, or undermines Coalition authority.

---

# SECTION IV — GENERAL CRIMINAL STATUTES

---

## §1 — Assault and Personal Violence

It is unlawful to strike, injure, threaten, restrain, poison, trap, ambush, or otherwise harm another person without lawful authority.

Enhanced penalties apply when the victim is:

- BWARS personnel
- NSIC personnel
- WXBRO personnel
- Facility management
- Tribunal staff
- Infrastructure workers
- Government contractors
- Registered witnesses

---

## §2 — Murder and Unauthorized Killing

The killing of any individual without sanctioned authority constitutes murder.

Murder may be classified as a Capital Offense where it involves:

- Government personnel
- Prison staff
- Informants
- Treasury personnel
- Witnesses
- Public officials
- Strategic facility workers

Unauthorized revenge killings, honor killings, gang executions, and vigilante actions remain prohibited.

---

## §3 — Threats Against Coalition Authority

Any threat against Coalition officials, facilities, symbols, districts, patrols, or strategic assets constitutes a Security Offense.

Threats need not be credible where they create public disruption, alarm, or symbolic challenge.

---

## §4 — Unlawful Assembly

Gatherings of three or more persons may be declared unlawful where such gathering:

- Obstructs movement
- Encourages dissent
- Blocks access
- Avoids registration
- Occurs near protected infrastructure
- Displays unauthorized symbols
- Appears coordinated without permit

Failure to disperse constitutes a Class A Offense.

---

## §5 — Curfew Violation

Movement during restricted hours without authorization is prohibited.

Curfew violations near:

- Banks
- Rail lines
- Government offices
- Prisons
- Power stations
- Storage yards
- Communication sites

may be elevated to a Security Offense.

---

## §6 — Unauthorized Entry

Entry into restricted, locked, sealed, abandoned, condemned, governmental, industrial, or subterranean areas without authorization is prohibited.

Possession of tools, maps, ladders, mining equipment, or false credentials may be treated as evidence of intent.

---

## §7 — Trespass on Strategic Property

Trespass upon Coalition strategic property constitutes a Security Offense.

Strategic property includes:

- Treasury sites
- BWARS installations
- NSIC facilities
- Utility corridors
- Rail systems
- Detention facilities
- Redstone systems
- Satellite uplink infrastructure
- Underground access shafts

---

## §8 — Theft

The unauthorized taking, possession, transfer, concealment, or destruction of property belonging to another person, company, faction, or the Coalition is prohibited.

Theft of Coalition property may be elevated automatically to an Economic Violation.

---

## §9 — Burglary

Entering a structure, facility, vault, apartment, business, warehouse, bunker, or secured room with intent to commit theft, sabotage, concealment, or unauthorized observation constitutes burglary.

Burglary of a public institution may be treated as Strategic Sabotage.

---

## §10 — Robbery

Theft involving force, intimidation, weapons, restraint, or implied threat constitutes robbery.

Robbery of reserve banks, treasury branches, resource depots, or government offices may be prosecuted under Economic Violations or High Treason statutes.

---

## §11 — Vandalism

Damage to structures, signs, public notices, roadways, cameras, lighting, banners, murals, checkpoints, or Coalition symbols is prohibited.

Defacement of official propaganda or government messaging is a Security Offense.

---

## §12 — Arson

The intentional ignition, burning, or attempted burning of any structure, field, storage area, vehicle, supply point, or infrastructure is prohibited.

Arson near strategic facilities may be classified as a Continuity Offense.

---

## §13 — Sabotage

Any deliberate interference with operational systems is prohibited.

Sabotage includes tampering with:

- Doors
- Elevators
- Rail switches
- Power relays
- Redstone systems
- Water lines
- Security cameras
- Vault mechanisms
- Communication equipment
- Surveillance nodes

Sabotage of strategic infrastructure may constitute High Treason.

---

## §14 — Possession of Burglary or Sabotage Tools

Possession of tools under suspicious circumstances may constitute an offense.

Restricted tools include:

- Lock tools
- Explosives
- Wire cutters
- Unauthorized access cards
- Redstone bypass devices
- Mining equipment near secured facilities
- Structural breaching tools
- Forged keys
- Concealed ladders

---

## §15 — False Identification

Possession, use, creation, sale, or transfer of false identity papers, badges, passes, keys, clearance tags, ration cards, or facility credentials is prohibited.

Use of false government credentials is a Tribunal Offense.

---

## §16 — Impersonation

Impersonating any Coalition employee, officer, inspector, contractor, soldier, administrator, tribunal member, or emergency worker is prohibited.

Impersonation of NSIC, BWARS, or WXBRO personnel constitutes a Security Offense.

---

## §17 — Obstruction of Authority

It is unlawful to obstruct, delay, mislead, evade, conceal from, or interfere with authorized personnel performing official duties.

Refusal to answer lawful questions may constitute obstruction.

---

## §18 — Failure to Comply

Failure to obey lawful orders issued by authorized personnel is prohibited.

Orders may include:

- Stop
- Identify yourself
- Open container
- Submit to inspection
- Leave area
- Remain in place
- Surrender property
- Report to station
- Cease recording
- Enter containment

---

## §19 — Resistance to Detainment

Resisting arrest, fleeing custody, hiding from patrols, assisting escape, or interfering with detainment constitutes a Class A Offense.

Escape from Tyrants Refuge or any protected detention site may be classified as High Treason.

---

## §20 — Harboring Offenders

Providing shelter, food, information, transport, documents, currency, materials, or warning to wanted persons is prohibited.

Harboring sanctioned persons may result in collective liability.

---

## §21 — Failure to Report

Citizens and personnel are required to report known or suspected:

- Treason
- Sabotage
- Weapons possession
- Unauthorized mining
- Drug activity
- Black-market trade
- Conspiracy
- OrCor contact
- NSIC leaks
- Suspicious excavation
- Anti-Coalition speech

Failure to report may be prosecuted as accessory conduct.

---

## §22 — Disorderly Conduct

Conduct disturbing public order is prohibited.

Disorderly conduct includes:

- Fighting
- Screaming
- Public intoxication
- Blocking roads
- Creating panic
- Loitering near checkpoints
- Provoking patrols
- Unauthorized preaching
- Public insults against authority
- Disturbing official ceremonies

---

## §23 — Public Insult Against Coalition Institutions

Statements, signs, gestures, writings, symbols, songs, chants, or displays intended to degrade Coalition legitimacy are prohibited.

Private criticism may be investigated where repeated, organized, or ideologically contagious.

---

## §24 — Unauthorized Publication

Printing, copying, distributing, broadcasting, posting, or displaying unauthorized materials is prohibited.

This includes:

- Pamphlets
- Maps
- Posters
- Leaked documents
- Anti-Coalition slogans
- Foreign propaganda
- Tribunal records
- Facility diagrams
- Patrol schedules
- Casualty lists

---

## §25 — Rumor Propagation

Spreading unverified information likely to undermine confidence in Coalition institutions is prohibited.

Rumors concerning:

- Treasury losses
- Executions
- military failures
- surveillance systems
- OrCor survival
- NSIC operations
- STARFIRE
- food shortages
- leadership disputes

may be treated as destabilizing conduct.

---

## §26 — Unauthorized Recording

Recording, sketching, mapping, photographing, copying, or documenting protected areas, personnel, vehicles, systems, or procedures without approval is prohibited.

Possession of such records is presumed unlawful unless authorization is shown.

---

## §27 — Surveillance Interference

Blocking, damaging, avoiding, spoofing, disabling, obstructing, or tampering with surveillance infrastructure is prohibited.

Wearing concealment for the purpose of avoiding identification may constitute a Security Offense.

---

## §28 — Loitering in Controlled Areas

Remaining without purpose near government buildings, rail lines, checkpoints, utility corridors, banks, prisons, substations, or restricted doors is prohibited.

Repeated loitering may trigger NSIC review.

---

## §29 — Unauthorized Movement Between Districts

Movement across district lines, security zones, industrial sectors, restricted neighborhoods, or curfew boundaries without documentation is prohibited.

Possession of multiple district passes may constitute identity fraud.

---

## §30 — Checkpoint Evasion

Avoiding, bypassing, tunneling around, climbing over, misleading, distracting, or fleeing from a checkpoint is prohibited.

Checkpoint evasion near restricted infrastructure may be treated as sabotage preparation.

---

## §31 — Illegal Tunneling

Digging, mining, expanding, concealing, or using tunnels without authorization is prohibited.

Tunnels beneath banks, prisons, towers, government offices, rail lines, or redstone infrastructure are presumed hostile.

---

## §32 — Unauthorized Construction

Building, modifying, expanding, demolishing, or fortifying any structure without permit is prohibited.

Unauthorized bunkers, walls, tunnels, hidden rooms, or observation posts are Security Offenses.

---

## §33 — Concealed Compartments

The creation or possession of hidden rooms, false walls, hidden chests, buried caches, secret ladders, or concealed access shafts without registration is prohibited.

Use of concealed storage near protected areas may constitute conspiracy.

---

## §34 — Possession of Restricted Materials

Possession of restricted materials without authorization is prohibited.

Restricted materials include:

- Yellorium
- Obsidian armor plating
- Military-grade redstone systems
- Explosive components
- Command keys
- Classified documents
- Encrypted ledgers
- Surveillance components
- Strategic ores
- Experimental devices

---

## §35 — Weapons Possession

Possession, manufacture, transfer, concealment, or use of weapons outside approved channels is prohibited.

Restricted weapons include:

- Firearms
- Explosives
- Poison devices
- Traps
- Military bows
- Heavy melee weapons
- Redstone weapon systems
- Autonomous defense systems

---

## §36 — Improvised Weapons

The construction or placement of improvised traps, mines, pressure-plate systems, concealed pits, lava devices, dispenser weapons, or similar hazards is prohibited.

Use against Coalition personnel constitutes a Capital Offense.

---

## §37 — Unauthorized Explosives

Possession, creation, storage, transport, or detonation of explosive material without authorization is prohibited.

Explosive offenses near infrastructure may be treated as High Treason.

---

## §38 — Unauthorized Armor or Uniforms

Possession or wearing of restricted armor, faction uniforms, BWARS-style clothing, NSIC insignia, military markings, or disguised tactical apparel is prohibited.

Unauthorized uniforms are presumed to enable infiltration.

---

## §39 — Illegal Communications

Unauthorized coded messages, encrypted channels, dead drops, signal lamps, hidden books, clandestine signs, or relay systems are prohibited.

Use of secret communication with sanctioned entities is a Security Offense.

---

## §40 — Interference With Communications

Jamming, cutting, redirecting, spoofing, intercepting, or falsifying communications is prohibited.

Interference with emergency broadcast or government channels may constitute High Treason.

---

## §41 — Possession of Classified Information

Possession of classified materials without authorization is prohibited regardless of intent.

Failure to surrender discovered classified material immediately constitutes unlawful retention.

---

## §42 — Unauthorized Archive Access

Entering, searching, copying, modifying, destroying, or removing archive material from government or facility records is prohibited.

Archive tampering may result in historical falsification charges.

---

## §43 — Data Tampering

Altering, deleting, hiding, encrypting, forging, or corrupting records, logs, maps, inventories, ledgers, personnel files, or surveillance records is prohibited.

Tampering with financial records may fall under Economic Violations.

---

## §44 — False Reporting

Knowingly providing false, incomplete, misleading, delayed, or strategically omitted reports to Coalition authorities is prohibited.

False reports involving treason, sabotage, weapons, treasury losses, or strategic systems may be elevated.

---

## §45 — Perjury

False testimony before tribunal, inquiry, audit, inspection, or security review is prohibited.

Perjury during classified proceedings may constitute High Treason.

---

## §46 — Bribery

Offering, accepting, soliciting, concealing, or facilitating unlawful benefit for official action is prohibited.

Bribery of security, tribunal, treasury, or intelligence personnel is a Security Offense.

---

## §47 — Corruption of Office

Officials who misuse authority, conceal violations, alter records, divert resources, protect criminals, or exploit position for private gain commit corruption of office.

Where corruption affects security, it may be prosecuted by tribunal.

---

## §48 — Negligence by Facility Leadership

Facility owners, officers, managers, and administrators are responsible for maintaining compliance.

Failures involving:

- Security
- Inventory
- surveillance
- reporting
- restricted access
- structural integrity
- employee discipline

may result in personal liability.

---

## §49 — Failure to Maintain Security

Failure to maintain locks, cameras, guard routes, lighting, barriers, alarms, ledgers, vault integrity, or access controls at protected facilities is prohibited.

Critical security failures may result in detention pending tribunal.

---

## §50 — Harboring Unauthorized Technology

Concealing, developing, testing, trading, or failing to disclose advanced or restricted technology is prohibited.

All discoveries with military, intelligence, resource, or strategic value must be reported.

---

## §51 — Unauthorized Research

Research involving weapons, energy systems, redstone automation, Yellorium, surveillance systems, tunneling methods, cryptography, or strategic materials without authorization is prohibited.

Unauthorized research may be treated as technological rebellion.

---

## §52 — Restricted Knowledge Distribution

Teaching, documenting, sharing, demonstrating, or publishing restricted techniques is prohibited.

Restricted techniques include:

- Vault bypass methods
- surveillance evasion
- strategic mining routes
- weapon construction
- checkpoint avoidance
- redstone sabotage
- code systems
- hidden room construction
- underground infiltration

---

## §53 — Conspiracy

Agreement, planning, preparation, signaling, resource gathering, reconnaissance, or role assignment toward any prohibited act constitutes conspiracy.

Completion of the intended act is not required.

---

## §54 — Attempt

Attempting any prohibited offense is punishable as the underlying offense where substantial steps are taken.

Possession of tools, plans, maps, or staged supplies may prove attempt.

---

## §55 — Accessory Conduct

Assisting, encouraging, funding, hiding, warning, distracting, transporting, or benefiting from an offense constitutes accessory conduct.

Accessory penalties may equal principal penalties.

---

## §56 — Organized Criminal Activity

Formation or participation in criminal groups, gangs, cells, black-market circles, unauthorized militias, dissident fronts, or illegal trade networks is prohibited.

Organized conduct may trigger collective sentencing.

---

## §57 — Unauthorized Militia Activity

Training, drilling, recruiting, arming, fortifying, or organizing armed groups outside Coalition approval is prohibited.

Unauthorized militia activity is presumed hostile.

---

## §58 — Illegal Foreign Contact

Contact with hostile, rogue, sanctioned, exiled, or unrecognized entities without authorization is prohibited.

OrCor-linked contact is automatically restricted.

---

## §59 — Aid to Rogue Factions

Providing resources, maps, shelter, labor, money, weapons, intelligence, or moral encouragement to rogue factions is prohibited.

Aid to sanctioned factions may constitute High Treason.

---

## §60 — Public Panic Creation

Actions or statements likely to cause public panic, market disruption, evacuation, hoarding, riot, or institutional distrust are prohibited.

Truth is not a defense where disclosure is unauthorized and destabilizing.

---

## §61 — Unregistered Labor Activity

Employment, contracting, collective work crews, private security, industrial labor, or repair services conducted outside approved registration systems are prohibited.

Labor networks may not operate independently of Coalition oversight.

---

## §62 — Work Refusal in Critical Sectors

Refusal to perform assigned labor in critical sectors during emergency declarations is prohibited.

Critical sectors include:

- Food
- power
- water
- construction
- transport
- security
- treasury
- communications
- mining
- detention support

---

## §63 — Sabotage by Neglect

Intentional underperformance, delay, tool misplacement, procedural noncompliance, or quiet disruption within critical operations may constitute sabotage by neglect.

---

## §64 — Unauthorized Trade

Trade outside registered markets, approved storefronts, ration systems, or licensed exchanges is prohibited.

Repeat violations may be prosecuted under Economic Violations.

---

## §65 — Ration Fraud

Forgery, duplication, resale, hoarding, alteration, or misuse of ration documents is prohibited.

Ration fraud during shortage conditions may be elevated to a Continuity Offense.

---

## §66 — Smuggling

Transporting goods, people, records, technology, weapons, drugs, or restricted resources through hidden routes or without inspection is prohibited.

Smuggling across district borders is a Security Offense.

---

## §67 — Contraband Possession

Possession of prohibited objects, writings, devices, substances, symbols, or unauthorized currency is prohibited.

Contraband may be seized without compensation.

---

## §68 — Public Intoxication

Appearing intoxicated in public, near work sites, near strategic facilities, during curfew, or while operating machinery is prohibited.

Intoxication is not a defense to criminal conduct.

---

## §69 — Drug Offenses

The Coalition prohibits unauthorized cultivation, production, possession, processing, distribution, trade, transport, storage, concealment, consumption, or promotion of controlled substances.

Controlled substances include:

- Hemp
- Hemp seeds
- Hemp fiber
- Hemp oils
- Hemp extracts
- Hemp resin
- Any hemp-derived byproduct
- Cocaine
- Coca leaves
- Coca extracts
- Stimulant powders
- Narcotic compounds
- Unregistered medical preparations
- Intoxicating agricultural products
- Unknown psychoactive substances

The following acts are prohibited:

- Cultivating hemp products
- Possessing hemp or any hemp byproduct
- Manufacturing hemp-derived materials
- Processing cocaine or coca-derived materials
- Possessing cocaine
- Transporting controlled substances
- Selling controlled substances
- Concealing controlled substances
- Permitting drug activity on one’s property
- Failing to report drug activity
- Promoting drug use
- Distributing drug-related instructions
- Maintaining drug production equipment

Drug offenses near schools, barracks, workplaces, industrial sites, detention facilities, or government structures may be elevated.

Cultivation of hemp or cocaine-related crops is presumed to indicate intent to distribute.

Drug activity involving Coalition personnel may trigger tribunal proceedings.

Property used in drug offenses is subject to seizure, demolition, or reassignment.

---

## §70 — Unauthorized Medicine

Producing, distributing, prescribing, possessing, or administering medicine outside approved medical channels is prohibited.

Unregistered medical practice may be treated as poisoning where harm occurs.

---

## §71 — Public Health Noncompliance

Failure to comply with quarantine, inspection, sanitation, ration-health, contamination, or emergency medical orders is prohibited.

Public health violations may authorize forced relocation.

---

## §72 — Desecration of Public Symbols

Defacing, burning, mocking, altering, removing, or damaging Coalition symbols, statues, banners, seals, signs, memorials, or approved historical markers is prohibited.

Desecration during public gathering may be treated as incitement.

---

## §73 — Unauthorized Religious or Ideological Assembly

Religious, philosophical, ideological, or educational gatherings require approval where they exceed private household activity or include organized doctrine.

Teachings conflicting with Coalition continuity may be restricted.

---

## §74 — Subversive Education

Instruction that weakens obedience, encourages unauthorized rights claims, promotes foreign systems, teaches resistance, or undermines Coalition history is prohibited.

Possession of unauthorized school materials may trigger inspection.

---

## §75 — Child Indoctrination Offenses

Teaching minors to distrust, disobey, mock, evade, or reject Coalition authority is prohibited.

Parents, guardians, teachers, and caretakers may be held liable.

---

## §76 — Unauthorized Migration or Settlement

Living, building, residing, relocating, or sheltering outside assigned districts without approval is prohibited.

Unauthorized settlements may be cleared without compensation.

---

## §77 — Vagrancy

Failure to maintain registered residence, employment, district assignment, or ration status is prohibited.

Unregistered persons may be detained until identity and loyalty status are verified.

---

## §78 — Harboring Unregistered Persons

Sheltering, employing, feeding, hiding, or transporting unregistered persons is prohibited.

Repeat offenses may indicate dissident support.

---

## §79 — Unauthorized Vehicle or Mount Operation

Operating carts, boats, aircraft, rail vehicles, mounts, or transport devices without approval is prohibited.

Unauthorized vehicle use during curfew is a Security Offense.

---

## §80 — Transport Route Violations

Blocking, damaging, diverting, or using restricted transport routes without authorization is prohibited.

Railway interference may constitute Strategic Sabotage.

---

## §81 — Environmental Damage

Unauthorized destruction of forests, water systems, farms, mines, terrain, protected landscapes, or resource zones is prohibited.

Environmental offenses may be treated as resource sabotage.

---

## §82 — Illegal Dumping

Dumping waste, hazardous material, contraband, bodies, defective equipment, or industrial byproducts without approval is prohibited.

Dumping near water, farms, or residential sectors may be elevated.

---

## §83 — Improper Burial or Body Concealment

Unauthorized burial, concealment, destruction, movement, or disposal of remains is prohibited.

Failure to report deaths immediately constitutes a criminal offense.

---

## §84 — Failure to Display Required Signage

Facilities must display required signs regarding ownership, establishment, commissioning, access level, safety, and inspection status.

Missing signage at regulated sites may result in closure.

---

## §85 — Falsification of Facility Status

Improperly marking a structure as authorized, commissioned, inspected, secure, public, private, abandoned, or restricted is prohibited.

False status markings near strategic sites are Security Offenses.

---

## §86 — Interference With Inspections

Delaying, obstructing, misleading, hiding materials from, or refusing inspectors is prohibited.

Inspection refusal authorizes forced entry.

---

## §87 — Unauthorized Animal Possession

Keeping, breeding, weaponizing, transporting, or releasing animals without approval is prohibited where such animals threaten public order, food systems, or transport.

---

## §88 — Noise and Signal Violations

Unauthorized alarms, sirens, horns, coded bells, repeated noise, signal fires, fireworks, lamps, or redstone pulses are prohibited.

Signals near restricted facilities may be treated as communication with hostile actors.

---

## §89 — Fire Safety Violations

Improper storage of fuel, explosives, wiring, lava, flammable blocks, redstone heat systems, or industrial fire sources is prohibited.

Facility managers are liable for unsafe fire conditions.

---

## §90 — Prisoner Contact

Unauthorized communication with prisoners, detainees, tribunal subjects, relocated persons, or Tyrants Refuge residents is prohibited.

Passing messages to detainees is a Security Offense.

---

## §91 — Witness Interference

Threatening, bribing, hiding, relocating, discrediting, or contacting witnesses without authorization is prohibited.

Witness interference in tribunal matters may constitute High Treason.

---

## §92 — Retaliation Against Informants

Retaliation against informants, suspected informants, cooperative witnesses, or reporting citizens is prohibited.

The Coalition does not require public identification of informants.

---

## §93 — Unauthorized Possession of Maps

Possession of maps showing restricted facilities, patrol routes, underground corridors, vault locations, safe houses, redstone lines, or surveillance blind spots is prohibited.

---

## §94 — Unauthorized Signal or Beacon Use

Constructing, lighting, powering, or transmitting from beacons, towers, antennas, lamps, or signal structures without approval is prohibited.

Signal structures may be seized immediately.

---

## §95 — Failure to Maintain Loyalty Documentation

Citizens, workers, and residents must maintain identification, work assignment, ration status, district authorization, and loyalty certification where applicable.

Failure to produce documentation upon request is prohibited.

---

## §96 — Administrative Evasion

Avoiding registration, refusing census, concealing household members, misreporting labor, altering addresses, or delaying official filings is prohibited.

---

## §97 — Collective Responsibility

Where offenses occur within a facility, household, unit, crew, or organization, responsible leadership may be held liable for failure to prevent, report, or correct misconduct.

Collective penalties may be authorized where individual responsibility cannot be isolated.

---

## §98 — Aggravating Conditions

Any offense may be enhanced where committed:

- During curfew
- During emergency
- Near protected infrastructure
- By government personnel
- With concealed identity
- With forged documents
- In coordination with others
- For ideological purposes
- To embarrass the Coalition
- To inspire imitation

---

## §99 — Emergency Expansion Clause

During declared emergency, unrest, war, sabotage event, treasury incident, WMD incident, or strategic continuity condition, Coalition authorities may temporarily expand, merge, or elevate criminal statutes as needed.

Public notice is not required where secrecy supports continuity.

Penalties may include:

- Warning citation
- Fine
- Confiscation
- Labor assignment
- Ration restriction
- Travel restriction
- Property seizure
- Facility closure
- Public correction order
- Detainment
- Relocation
- Tribunal referral

Sentencing shall prioritize deterrence, public order, and continuity preservation.

Disorder spreads when tolerated.

A single unreported offense may become a network.
A single unlicensed tunnel may become a breach.
A single rumor may become a riot.
A single hidden room may become a command post.

Order is maintained by prevention.
