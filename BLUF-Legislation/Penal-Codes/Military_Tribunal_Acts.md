# BLUF LEGISLATIVE FRAMEWORK
## PENAL CODE

DOCUMENT: MILITARY TRIBUNAL ACTS  
CLASSIFICATION: INTERNAL LEGAL REFERENCE  
STATUS: ACTIVE  
REVISION: 3.1  
DATE OF ISSUE: 2025-03-08

---

# SECTION I — PURPOSE

The Military Tribunal Acts establish the legal framework governing Coalition military judicial proceedings, emergency wartime adjudication, continuity enforcement hearings, and strategic security prosecutions.

These statutes authorize expedited tribunal authority where conventional judicial processes are determined insufficient for preservation of Coalition stability.

---

# SECTION II — JURISDICTION

Military tribunals may exercise authority over:

- BWARS personnel
- NSIC personnel
- WXBRO officers
- Coalition contractors
- Sanctioned civilians
- Dissident actors
- Foreign hostile entities
- Individuals accused of continuity-threatening offenses

Jurisdiction may extend beyond standard territorial boundaries under emergency authority declarations.

---

# SECTION III — AUTHORIZED TRIBUNAL CONDITIONS

A military tribunal may be convened under any of the following conditions:

- Active insurrection
- Strategic sabotage
- High Treason investigations
- Unauthorized weapons development
- Wartime emergency declarations
- Classified information breaches
- Continuity-threatening activity
- Internal security failures
- Large-scale civil instability

NSIC Omega may recommend immediate tribunal authorization where delay presents operational risk.

---

# SECTION IV — TRIBUNAL COMPOSITION

Military tribunals shall consist of:

- One presiding tribunal officer
- Two Coalition adjudicators
- One BWARS or NSIC security representative
- Authorized record personnel where applicable

Clearance restrictions may limit visibility of proceedings to compartmentalized attendees only.

Certain proceedings may occur without public acknowledgment.

---

# SECTION V — PROCEDURAL AUTHORITY

Military tribunals are authorized to:

- Conduct closed hearings
- Restrict legal representation
- Seal evidence
- Classify testimony
- Suppress operational details
- Expedite sentencing
- Deny public disclosure
- Issue emergency rulings

Traditional civilian evidentiary standards may be suspended where continuity concerns outweigh procedural limitations.

---

# SECTION VI — CLASSIFIED PROCEEDINGS

Proceedings involving:

- Orbital systems
- NSIC operations
- Strategic weapons
- Intelligence assets
- Continuity infrastructure
- Surveillance architecture
- Compartmentalized directives

shall automatically qualify for classified tribunal status.

Unauthorized disclosure of tribunal proceedings constitutes a criminal offense under High Treason statutes.

---

# SECTION VII — DETENTION AUTHORITY

Individuals awaiting tribunal proceedings may be relocated to:

- Tyrants Refuge
- NSIC containment sectors
- BWARS detention facilities
- Underground holding complexes
- Classified relocation sites

Detainment duration may be extended indefinitely under continuity review authority.

---

# SECTION VIII — SENTENCING AUTHORITY

Military tribunals may authorize:

- Imprisonment
- Forced labor assignment
- Asset confiscation
- Operational exile
- Clearance revocation
- Identity suppression
- Historical erasure
- Execution

Sentencing severity shall consider:

- Threat level
- Operational damage
- Public destabilization risk
- Information exposure
- Possibility of ideological spread

---

# SECTION IX — EMERGENCY CONTINUITY POWERS

During declared continuity emergencies, tribunal authority supersedes standard civilian judicial systems.

Under emergency conditions:

- Appeals may be suspended
- Public trials may be prohibited
- Immediate sentencing may be authorized
- Containment actions may proceed without public filing

Continuity preservation remains the primary objective of all tribunal operations.

---

# SECTION X — HISTORICAL RECORD CONTROL

The Coalition reserves authority to:

- Seal tribunal outcomes
- Reclassify proceedings
- Alter public summaries
- Restrict archival access
- Remove individuals from official records

Public-facing reports may differ from classified operational findings where necessary for societal stability.

---

# STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

The Military Tribunal system exists not solely to punish offenses, but to preserve continuity during periods where instability threatens governmental survival.

Order cannot depend entirely upon transparency.

Certain threats require resolution beyond public observation.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

Justice preserves law. Tribunals preserve governments.
