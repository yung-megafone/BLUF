# SECTION I — PURPOSE

The Economic Violations statutes establish prohibited financial, commercial, industrial, and resource-related conduct determined to threaten Coalition economic stability, centralized authority, or strategic continuity.

The Coalition recognizes economic control as a foundational component of territorial governance and public order.

Acts undermining economic structure shall therefore be treated as threats to continuity itself.

---

# SECTION II — DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this document:

"Economic Infrastructure" includes:
- Treasury systems
- Reserve banks
- Resource stockpiles
- Industrial production facilities
- Coalition-approved trade networks
- Transportation logistics systems
- Energy generation infrastructure
- Authorized financial exchanges

"Unauthorized Commerce" refers to any economic activity conducted outside Coalition-recognized oversight mechanisms.

---

# SECTION III — MAJOR ECONOMIC OFFENSES

---

## §3.1 Theft of Coalition Assets

The unauthorized acquisition, diversion, concealment, or destruction of:

- Coalition currency
- Strategic reserves
- Treasury holdings
- Government property
- Industrial resources
- Restricted materials

shall constitute a major economic offense.

Where losses exceed continuity thresholds, charges may escalate to Strategic Sabotage or High Treason.

---

## §3.2 Black-Market Trade

Participation in unregistered or prohibited trade activity involving:

- Restricted technologies
- Weapons systems
- Strategic resources
- Classified materials
- Surveillance equipment
- Yellorium
- Coalition ration goods

is prohibited without explicit authorization.

Confiscation authority is automatic upon discovery.

---

## §3.3 Resource Hoarding

Intentional accumulation or concealment of essential resources in quantities determined to threaten public distribution stability may constitute economic destabilization.

Relevant materials include:

- Fuel
- Industrial components
- Construction materials
- Food reserves
- Energy resources
- Rare minerals

Emergency continuity declarations may expand restricted categories.

---

## §3.4 Financial Fraud

The following constitute prohibited financial activity:

- Counterfeit currency production
- Ledger manipulation
- Unauthorized reserve transfers
- Treasury falsification
- Shell accounting concealment
- Fraudulent procurement activity
- Strategic insurance exploitation

Coalition reserve institutions are considered protected infrastructure.

---

## §3.5 Unauthorized Excavation and Mining

Mining, tunneling, excavation, or underground resource extraction conducted without Coalition approval is prohibited within:

- Restricted districts
- Treasury-adjacent sectors
- Strategic infrastructure zones
- NSIC-controlled territories
- Underground transit corridors

Subsurface activity near critical infrastructure may be treated as attempted sabotage.

---

## §3.6 Industrial Negligence

Operators of industrial or financial facilities found responsible for:

- Security failures
- Improper storage practices
- Procedural negligence
- Surveillance gaps
- Unauthorized access vulnerabilities

may face tribunal proceedings under continuity risk statutes.

Leadership personnel may be held personally liable for resulting damages.

---

## §3.7 Unauthorized Independent Economies

The creation of unapproved economic structures including:

- Parallel currencies
- Independent taxation systems
- Unregistered trade hubs
- Autonomous industrial zones
- Non-Coalition reserve systems

constitutes an attack on centralized continuity authority.

---

# SECTION IV — ENFORCEMENT AUTHORITY

The following entities maintain enforcement jurisdiction:

- BWARS Economic Security Division
- Treasury Oversight Bureau
- NSIC Financial Intelligence Cells
- Coalition Tribunal Authorities
- Authorized WXBRO audit personnel

Emergency authority expansions may be enacted during periods of instability.

---

# SECTION V — INVESTIGATIVE POWERS

Authorized investigators may:

- Freeze assets
- Seize inventories
- Monitor transactions
- Conduct financial audits
- Restrict trade access
- Shut down facilities
- Monitor communications
- Establish economic checkpoints

Classified financial investigations may proceed without public notification.

---

# SECTION VI — PENALTIES

Economic violations may result in:

- Monetary seizure
- Imprisonment
- Forced labor assignment
- Facility confiscation
- Trade prohibition
- Citizenship restrictions
- Historical record sanctions
- Tribunal escalation

Where economic crimes threaten Coalition continuity, sentencing may escalate to High Treason classifications.

---

# SECTION VII — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

The Coalition recognizes economic stability as inseparable from governmental authority.

Financial systems are not merely markets, but instruments of territorial control, logistical coordination, and public order.

Unregulated economies create unregulated loyalties.

---

Personnel are reminded:

A government that loses control of its currency eventually loses control of its territory.
