## HAZARDOUS MATERIALS STORAGE AND CONTAINMENT ACT

DOCUMENT ID: LF-2025-011  
SUBJECT: STORAGE OF HAZARDOUS, RADIOACTIVE, AND BIOLOGICAL MATERIALS  
CLASSIFICATION: REGULATORY FRAMEWORK  
STATUS: ACTIVE  
DATE OF ISSUE: 2025-03-14

---

# SECTION I — PURPOSE

This framework establishes mandatory Coalition standards governing the possession, transportation, containment, transfer, storage, documentation, and disposal of hazardous, radioactive, biological, chemical, and strategically dangerous materials within all Coalition-controlled territory.

The Coalition recognizes uncontrolled hazardous material proliferation as a direct threat to:

- Public safety
- Infrastructure continuity
- Environmental stability
- Strategic security
- Economic function
- Population survivability

As such, all regulated materials shall remain subject to centralized oversight authority.

---

# SECTION II — AUTHORIZED STORAGE FACILITY

All hazardous, radioactive, biological, chemical, and strategic contamination-risk materials must be stored exclusively at:

## SafHaz RadiSafe Facility
30 Freedom Dr.  
Coalition Strategic Containment Zone

The SafHaz RadiSafe Facility is recognized as the sole primary authorized long-term storage and containment authority for regulated materials under Coalition jurisdiction.

No secondary facility may retain regulated materials without explicit written authorization from Coalition Continuity Oversight and NSIC Hazard Divisions.

---

# SECTION III — REGULATED MATERIALS

The following materials are regulated under this framework:

---

## §3.1 Radioactive Materials

Including but not limited to:

- Yellorium
- Reactor byproducts
- Irradiated industrial components
- Enriched fuel materials
- Experimental energy compounds
- Contaminated storage containers
- Radioactive mining outputs

---

## §3.2 Biological Materials

Including but not limited to:

- Engineered biological agents
- Experimental organisms
- Restricted fungal cultures
- Hazardous agricultural pathogens
- Contaminated tissue samples
- Bio-reactive compounds
- Genetic research materials

---

## §3.3 Chemical Materials

Including but not limited to:

- Industrial toxins
- Reactive compounds
- Corrosive substances
- Weaponizable chemicals
- Toxic gas precursors
- Laboratory solvents
- Restricted explosive compounds

---

## §3.4 Strategic Hazard Materials

Including:

- Unknown experimental substances
- OrCor-derived research remnants
- NSIC containment samples
- Restricted energy systems
- Classified compounds
- Materials designated hazardous by emergency order

---

# SECTION IV — STORAGE REQUIREMENTS

All regulated materials must:

- Be logged within Coalition inventory systems
- Remain sealed in approved containment systems
- Be traceable through transport records
- Remain accessible for inspection
- Be separated by hazard classification
- Utilize approved shielding systems
- Maintain environmental containment compliance

Underground concealment, private storage, hidden vaults, or unauthorized laboratory retention are prohibited.

---

# SECTION V — TRANSPORTATION REQUIREMENTS

Transport of regulated materials requires:

- Coalition transport authorization
- BWARS escort where applicable
- Registered shipment documentation
- Sealed containment systems
- Route approval
- Emergency containment planning

Unauthorized transport constitutes a Security Offense.

Transport routes may remain classified.

---

# SECTION VI — FACILITY RESPONSIBILITIES

Facilities handling regulated materials are required to:

- Immediately disclose hazardous inventory
- Report containment failures
- Report theft or diversion
- Maintain inspection readiness
- Maintain environmental safeguards
- Cooperate with NSIC and BWARS inspections

Failure to report containment breach conditions may constitute criminal negligence or Strategic Sabotage.

---

# SECTION VII — EMERGENCY RESPONSE AUTHORITY

Where contamination, biological release, radiation leak, unauthorized experimentation, or hazardous material theft is suspected, Coalition authorities may authorize:

- Immediate quarantine
- Forced evacuation
- Facility seizure
- Communications blackout
- Personnel detainment
- Infrastructure lockdown
- Emergency containment operations

Public disclosure may be delayed where panic risk exceeds informational necessity.

---

# SECTION VIII — INSPECTION AUTHORITY

The following entities may conduct inspections:

- BWARS Hazard Enforcement Division
- NSIC Hazard Containment Authority
- Treasury Resource Oversight
- Coalition Environmental Stability Bureau
- Authorized WXBRO inspection teams

Inspections may occur without prior notice.

Refusal of inspection authorizes forced entry.

---

# SECTION IX — PROHIBITED CONDUCT

The following are prohibited:

- Unauthorized possession of regulated materials
- Concealment of hazardous inventory
- Illegal experimentation
- Improper disposal
- Unauthorized sale or transfer
- Off-site storage
- Diversion of containment shipments
- Construction of unregistered laboratories
- Tampering with containment systems

Violations involving radioactive or strategic materials may constitute High Treason.

---

# SECTION X — LIABILITY

Facility owners, operators, managers, researchers, contractors, and transport personnel may be held personally liable for:

- Containment failures
- Exposure incidents
- Environmental contamination
- Unauthorized transfers
- Missing inventory
- Structural damage caused by regulated materials

Liability extends beyond operational termination.

---

# SECTION XI — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

The Coalition recognizes hazardous materials not merely as industrial assets, but as continuity-level risks requiring centralized control.

No private entity may possess strategic contamination capability independent of Coalition oversight.

Containment is considered a matter of governmental survival.

---
