# PURPOSE

This directive defines emergency powers available to the Coalition during events that threaten governmental continuity, territorial stability, strategic infrastructure, public order, or survival of the state.

---

# SECTION I — DECLARATION AUTHORITY

## §1.1 High Table Authority

The High Table may declare a Continuity Emergency when ordinary governance is insufficient to preserve order.

---

## §1.2 Emergency Recommendation

BWARS, NSIC, WXBRO, Treasury Authority, or Regional Command may recommend emergency declaration.

NSIC may issue temporary sealed emergency recommendations where classified threats exist.

---

# SECTION II — QUALIFYING CONDITIONS

A Continuity Emergency may be declared during:

- Invasion
- Insurrection
- Treasury collapse
- WMD incident
- Strategic sabotage
- Mass unrest
- Infrastructure failure
- Hazard release
- Orbital threat condition
- Classified intelligence breach
- Collapse of district authority

---

# SECTION III — AUTHORIZED POWERS

During a Continuity Emergency, the Coalition may:

- Suspend movement rights
- Enforce curfew
- Restrict communication
- Expand surveillance
- Freeze assets
- Requisition property
- Relocate civilians
- Seal districts
- Expand tribunal authority
- Nationalize infrastructure
- Restrict publication
- Detain high-risk persons
- Deploy militarized BWARS units

---

# SECTION IV — DISTRICT CONTROL

Districts may be placed under emergency administration.

Emergency administrators may:

- Override local policy
- Command security assets
- Suspend public gatherings
- Restrict commerce
- Order evacuations
- Close transportation routes
- Seize supplies

---

# SECTION V — INFORMATION CONTROL

Public disclosure of emergency causes, damages, casualties, strategic failures, or classified risks may be restricted.

Public narratives may be adjusted to prevent panic.

---

# SECTION VI — DURATION

Emergency powers remain active until the High Table determines continuity has been restored.

No maximum duration applies where instability continues.

---

# SECTION VII — REVIEW

Emergency powers may be reviewed internally by the High Table.

Public challenge is not required during active emergency conditions.

---

# CLOSING DOCTRINE

Temporary authority may become lasting authority where instability remains unresolved.

Continuity does not expire while threats persist.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A government that waits for permission to survive has already begun to fail.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
