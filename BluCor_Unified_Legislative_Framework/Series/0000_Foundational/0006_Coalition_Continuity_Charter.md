# DIRECTIVE 0006

## COALITION CONTINUITY CHARTER

### Foundational Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L0`  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This charter defines continuity preservation as the supreme operational duty of the [[1102_BluCor_Administration_Charter|BluCor]] Strategic Coalition.

All agencies, commanders, administrators, and authorities shall prioritize continuity where ordinary governance fails.

---

# SECTION I — CONTINUITY DEFINITION

Continuity means preservation of:

- Command authority
- Legal authority
- Territorial control
- Strategic infrastructure
- Treasury systems
- Intelligence records
- Enforcement capacity
- Population stability
- Historical record integrity

Continuity may survive even where normal administration is disrupted.

---

# SECTION II — CONTINUITY AUTHORITIES

The following institutions are recognized as continuity-bearing authorities:

- High Table
- [[1150_WXBRO_Strategic_Oversight|WXBRO]] personnel
- [[1101_BWARS_Authority_Doctrine|BWARS]] command
- [[1112_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] command
- [[1300_Treasury_Authority_Charter|Treasury Authority]]
- Regional Command
- Strategic infrastructure administrators

Authority succession shall be determined by classified and public continuity schedules.

---

# SECTION III — SUCCESSION

Where primary authority becomes unavailable, succession may proceed through:

- High Table deputies
- WXBRO continuity officers
- Regional command structures
- [[1101_BWARS_Authority_Doctrine|BWARS]] emergency command
- [[1112_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] sealed command channels
- Treasury continuity administrators

Public disclosure of succession hierarchy is not required.

---

# SECTION IV — RECORD PRESERVATION

Continuity requires preservation of:

- Constitutional documents
- Penal statutes
- Agency charters
- Treasury ledgers
- Classified directives
- Maps
- Personnel registries
- Strategic access records

Records may be relocated, sealed, duplicated, or destroyed to prevent hostile capture.

---

# SECTION V — INFRASTRUCTURE PRESERVATION

Continuity infrastructure includes:

- Command facilities
- Treasury vaults
- Communications arrays
- Power systems
- Rail corridors
- Underground shelters
- Archives
- Surveillance systems
- Detention facilities

Infrastructure preservation may justify emergency seizure or relocation.

---

# SECTION VI — POPULATION STABILITY

The Coalition shall preserve population stability through:

- Housing control
- Food distribution
- Movement regulation
- Labor assignment
- Emergency relocation
- Security enforcement
- Information control

Uncontrolled population movement is a continuity risk.

---

# SECTION VII — CLASSIFIED CONTINUITY

Some continuity plans may remain permanently classified.

Such plans may involve:

- Alternate command sites
- Hidden infrastructure
- Emergency archives
- Strategic weapons
- Denial protocols
- Compartmentalized personnel movement

Absence of public knowledge does not imply absence of authority.

---

# SECTION VIII — CONTINUITY OVER NORMALCY

During crisis, continuity supersedes:

- Administrative convenience
- Civil preference
- Local authority
- Property rights
- Public transparency
- Standard judicial timing

Normal governance may resume only after continuity is secured.

---

# CLOSING DOCTRINE

A government is not measured by comfort during peace.

It is measured by whether it survives disorder.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

Continuity is not a department.  
Continuity is the reason departments exist.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
