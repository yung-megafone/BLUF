# PURPOSE

This directive establishes the recognized civil guarantees, civic duties, and limitation clauses applicable to citizens, residents, registered workers, and authorized persons within Coalition territory.

These rights are conditional upon public order, legal compliance, and continuity preservation.

---

# PREAMBLE

The Coalition recognizes that stable governance requires defined expectations between authority and population.

Rights exist to stabilize society, not to destabilize it.

---

# ARTICLE I — RIGHT TO EXISTENCE

Registered citizens and lawful residents possess the right to reside within Coalition territory provided they remain compliant with Coalition law.

This right may be suspended under:

- Tribunal sentence
- Quarantine order
- Security relocation
- Treason investigation
- Continuity emergency
- District exclusion order

---

# ARTICLE II — RIGHT TO SHELTER

Citizens shall have access to approved housing where available.

Housing may be assigned, inspected, relocated, or requisitioned under lawful authority.

Unauthorized settlements, hidden dwellings, and unregistered underground residences are not protected.

---

# ARTICLE III — RIGHT TO FOOD AND WATER

The Coalition shall maintain systems for food and water access during normal and emergency conditions.

Distribution may be prioritized according to:

- Labor classification
- Security function
- Medical necessity
- Infrastructure role
- Emergency conditions

---

# ARTICLE IV — RIGHT TO LABOR

Citizens may participate in approved labor systems.

During emergencies, labor may be redirected toward critical sectors including:

- Power
- Food
- Construction
- Security
- Transportation
- Containment
- Communications

Refusal of emergency labor orders is not protected.

---

# ARTICLE V — RIGHT TO MEDICAL ACCESS

Citizens may access approved medical treatment through authorized providers.

Unauthorized medicine, biological experimentation, unregistered treatment, and illicit pharmaceuticals remain prohibited.

---

# ARTICLE VI — RIGHT TO MOVEMENT

Citizens may move within authorized districts subject to:

- Curfew
- Checkpoints
- District permits
- Security zones
- Emergency closures
- Strategic infrastructure exclusions

Unrestricted movement is not guaranteed.

---

# ARTICLE VII — RIGHT TO EXPRESSION

Citizens may express opinions on civic life provided such expression does not:

- Encourage unrest
- Reveal classified information
- Undermine continuity
- Support hostile factions
- Spread panic
- Interfere with lawful operations

Expression may be restricted where public order requires.

---

# ARTICLE VIII — RIGHT TO ASSEMBLY

Citizens may assemble peacefully where authorized.

Assemblies may be restricted, dispersed, or prohibited where they:

- Obstruct movement
- Challenge continuity authority
- Spread panic
- Interfere with infrastructure
- Occur near restricted sites
- Exceed emergency limits

---

# ARTICLE IX — RIGHT TO PROPERTY

Citizens may possess personal property subject to Coalition regulation.

The Coalition may inspect, seize, requisition, restrict, or demolish property where necessary for:

- Security
- Containment
- Infrastructure protection
- Emergency relocation
- Economic stability
- Tribunal enforcement

Strategic materials are not protected private property.

---

# ARTICLE X — RIGHT TO DUE PROCESS

Accused persons may receive review before authorized judicial, administrative, or tribunal authority.

Classified proceedings may occur where public disclosure would threaten continuity.

---

# ARTICLE XI — RIGHT TO PETITION

Citizens may submit grievances, requests, appeals, and administrative corrections through approved channels.

Petitions advocating separatism, treason, sabotage, or foreign intervention are prohibited.

---

# ARTICLE XII — RIGHT TO INFORMATION

Citizens may access information approved for public release.

Classified, restricted, sealed, or compartmentalized material is exempt.

Truth alone does not compel disclosure.

---

# ARTICLE XIII — RIGHT TO SECURITY

The Coalition shall maintain security institutions to protect citizens from:

- Crime
- Rogue factions
- Sabotage
- Invasion
- Economic collapse
- Hazard exposure
- Infrastructure failure

Security measures may include surveillance, inspection, curfew, and movement regulation.

---

# ARTICLE XIV — LIMITATIONS CLAUSE

All rights are subordinate to:

- Coalition continuity
- Public order
- Strategic security
- Infrastructure survival
- Classified operations
- Emergency authority

Rights may be suspended where continuity demands.

---

# CLOSING DOCTRINE

Rights are recognized because order exists.

Order exists because authority remains capable of enforcing it.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Citizens are reminded:

Freedom exists inside the perimeter.  
The perimeter exists because freedom cannot survive unguarded.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
