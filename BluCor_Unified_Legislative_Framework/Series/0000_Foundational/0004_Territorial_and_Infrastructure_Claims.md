# PURPOSE

This directive establishes Coalition territorial claims, infrastructure sovereignty, subterranean jurisdiction, strategic city designations, and protected development zones.

---

# SECTION I — TERRITORIAL AUTHORITY

## §1.1 Recognized Territory

Coalition territory includes all lands:

- Occupied
- Developed
- Excavated
- Fortified
- Supplied
- Connected
- Administered
- Defended

by Coalition authority since the Foundational Era.

---

## §1.2 Abandoned Territory

Territory is not surrendered through abandonment, vacancy, damage, temporary evacuation, or lack of civilian presence.

A damaged district remains Coalition territory.

---

# SECTION II — VERTICAL SOVEREIGNTY

## §2.1 Surface Authority

Coalition jurisdiction applies across all recognized surface territory.

---

## §2.2 Underground Authority

Coalition jurisdiction extends vertically beneath all recognized territory.

This includes:

- Tunnels
- Vaults
- Bunkers
- Rail systems
- Utility corridors
- Deep residences
- Hidden chambers
- Subterranean command sectors

Unauthorized underground development constitutes territorial encroachment.

---

## §2.3 Airspace and Orbital Authority

Coalition sovereignty extends to controlled airspace, signal infrastructure, launch corridors, orbital uplink systems, and strategic observation zones.

Unauthorized aerial or orbital development is prohibited.

---

# SECTION III — STRATEGIC CITY DESIGNATION

Strategic City status may be applied to locations containing:

- Administrative command centers
- Treasury infrastructure
- Detention infrastructure
- Strategic residential density
- Underground systems
- Orbital uplinks
- Military command facilities

Strategic Cities may receive expanded surveillance and security authority.

---

# SECTION IV — BORDERLANDS

Borderland sectors are controlled frontier regions requiring enhanced security and monitoring.

The Southwest Borderlands are recognized as a strategic border and weapons testing region.

---

# SECTION V — INFRASTRUCTURE CLAIMS

The Coalition reserves authority over:

- Rail lines
- Power stations
- Communications networks
- Surveillance systems
- Roadways
- Bridges
- Hazard containment sites
- Treasury vaults
- Strategic archives
- Military installations

Infrastructure remains Coalition property where built, funded, seized, or maintained by Coalition authority.

---

# SECTION VI — MAPS AND DISCLOSURE

Public maps may omit, simplify, falsify, or restrict certain infrastructure where full disclosure threatens security.

Restricted territorial overlays may remain classified.

---

# CLOSING DOCTRINE

Territory is not merely land.

Territory is infrastructure, surveillance, access control, and the ability to enforce authority.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A border drawn on paper is a claim.  
A border watched by infrastructure is sovereignty.
