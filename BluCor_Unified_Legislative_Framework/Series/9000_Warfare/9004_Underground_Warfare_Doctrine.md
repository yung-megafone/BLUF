# PURPOSE

This directive establishes Coalition doctrine for defensive and offensive operations within subterranean infrastructure, including tunnels, vault systems, bunkers, rail corridors, service shafts, deep residential sectors, and continuity facilities.

The Coalition recognizes underground space as both refuge and vulnerability.

---

# SECTION I — SUBTERRANEAN AUTHORITY

All underground systems beneath Coalition territory fall under BSC jurisdiction regardless of surface ownership.

Unauthorized underground expansion is prohibited.

---

# SECTION II — DEFENSIVE USE

Underground infrastructure shall support:

- Emergency shelter
- Tactical withdrawal
- Personnel relocation
- Hidden logistics
- Command continuity
- Medical staging
- Archive preservation
- Supply retention

Critical underground sectors shall remain segmented to prevent total compromise.

---

# SECTION III — HOSTILE TUNNELING

Hostile tunnel activity includes:

- Unauthorized excavation
- Hidden access corridors
- Vault approach tunnels
- Prison breach tunnels
- Surveillance bypass routes
- Borderland infiltration tunnels

Discovery of hostile tunneling authorizes immediate lockdown and structural denial review.

---

# SECTION IV — CONTROL MEASURES

Underground sectors should maintain:

- Checkpoints
- Blast doors
- Manual lockouts
- Surveillance corridors
- Emergency lighting
- Redundant access routes
- Sealed maintenance hatches
- Compartmentalized maps

Maps of deep systems shall remain restricted.

---

# SECTION V — DENIAL DOCTRINE

Where underground facilities risk capture, authorized commanders may order:

- Sector sealing
- Corridor collapse
- Power denial
- Flood isolation
- Access destruction
- Route abandonment
- Archive evacuation

Preservation of secrecy may outweigh preservation of structure.

---

# SECTION VI — CIVILIAN UNDERGROUND AREAS

Residential basements, underground apartments, public tunnels, and shelter zones shall remain subject to inspection.

Civilian underground areas may be requisitioned during emergencies.

---

# SECTION VII — STRATEGIC JUSTIFICATION

Surface governments fall when their streets fall.

Continuity governments survive below the streets.

The Coalition shall maintain underground depth sufficient to outlast surface disruption.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

The surface is what the enemy sees.  
The underground is what the Coalition keeps.

END OF DIRECTIVE
