# PURPOSE

This directive establishes standardized Coalition doctrine governing tactical response, territorial stabilization, infrastructure denial, surveillance integration, and escalation procedures throughout recognized Coalition borderland sectors.

The Coalition recognizes borderlands as inherently unstable environments vulnerable to:

* Infiltration
* Smuggling
* Insurgency
* Unauthorized settlement
* Reconnaissance activity
* Resource theft
* Strategic encroachment
* Dissident organization

Border territories shall therefore remain under enhanced operational oversight.

---

# SECTION I — BORDERLAND CLASSIFICATION

## §1.1 Definition of Borderlands

Borderlands are defined as territorial sectors located near:

* Coalition territorial edges
* Restricted strategic regions
* Unstable neighboring sectors
* Unregulated wilderness
* Missile testing zones
* Industrial exclusion regions
* NSIC containment boundaries
* Unmapped subterranean sectors

Borderlands may include both surface and underground operational areas.

---

## §1.2 Strategic Importance

Borderlands serve as:

* Defensive buffers
* Observation zones
* Strategic warning regions
* Controlled denial sectors
* Testing environments
* Intelligence collection corridors

Coalition doctrine recognizes border instability as preferable to uncontrolled hostile penetration into central districts.

---

# SECTION II — PATROL DOCTRINE

## §2.1 Persistent Presence

BWARS patrol visibility shall remain intentionally unpredictable throughout border sectors.

Patrol doctrine shall include:

* Randomized movement routes
* Irregular timing
* Elevated observation posts
* Hidden monitoring positions
* Mobile checkpoints
* Temporary roadblocks
* Rotating personnel assignments

Predictable patrol behavior is prohibited.

---

## §2.2 Layered Patrol Zones

Border sectors shall operate under layered patrol depth systems.

Zones may include:

### Green Zone

General patrol territory with standard monitoring.

### Yellow Zone

Elevated monitoring sector with increased inspection authority.

### Red Zone

Restricted tactical response area where hostile activity is presumed likely.

### Black Zone

Continuity-sensitive sector authorized for lethal force escalation without standard warning requirements.

Black Zone designation remains compartmentalized where practical.

---

## §2.3 Observation Infrastructure

Border regions should incorporate:

* Watchtowers
* Hidden observation rooms
* Elevated sniper positions
* Rail monitoring points
* Motion-triggered alert systems
* Signal relays
* Underground observation corridors

Surveillance overlap is considered essential.

---

# SECTION III — ACCESS CONTROL

## §3.1 Border Crossing Authority

Movement across border sectors requires:

* Valid identification
* Travel authorization
* Cargo inspection
* BWARS clearance where applicable

Unauthorized crossings may constitute hostile infiltration.

---

## §3.2 Vehicle and Cargo Inspections

BWARS personnel may inspect:

* Rail cargo
* Industrial shipments
* Underground freight
* Fuel transports
* Construction materials
* Excavation equipment
* Communications systems

Cargo concealment methods are presumed suspicious.

---

## §3.3 Underground Monitoring

All known underground access routes within border sectors shall remain subject to:

* Structural mapping
* Tunnel inspections
* Collapse contingency planning
* Seismic monitoring
* Thermal surveillance
* Patrol integration

Unknown subterranean expansion is considered a continuity threat.

---

# SECTION IV — RESPONSE TO HOSTILE ACTIVITY

## §4.1 Escalation Doctrine

Response escalation shall proceed according to operational threat level.

Authorized escalation stages include:

1. Observation
2. Identification
3. Tracking
4. Detainment attempt
5. Tactical interception
6. Area lockdown
7. Lethal engagement
8. Infrastructure denial

Escalation may be accelerated where strategic infrastructure is threatened.

---

## §4.2 Ambush and Encirclement

Coalition border doctrine prioritizes:

* Chokepoint control
* Forced movement corridors
* Encirclement routes
* Elevated engagement positions
* Tunnel collapse denial
* Restricted escape vectors

Hostile entities should be redirected rather than pursued blindly.

---

## §4.3 Borderland Denial Tactics

Authorized denial measures include:

* Controlled bridge destruction
* Rail disablement
* Tunnel collapse
* Barrier deployment
* Floodgate activation
* Area ignition
* Redstone shutdown
* Controlled crater creation

Infrastructure denial may be authorized before territorial loss becomes imminent.

---

# SECTION V — INSURGENCY AND DISSIDENT RESPONSE

## §5.1 Dissident Suppression

Borderlands are recognized as likely regions for dissident organization.

BWARS and NSIC personnel may therefore authorize:

* Expanded surveillance
* Informant cultivation
* Communications interception
* Controlled misinformation
* Safehouse raids
* Restricted movement orders

Unauthorized camps or fortified structures may be treated as insurgent positions.

---

## §5.2 Population Control

Population movement within unstable border sectors may be restricted through:

* Curfews
* Checkpoints
* Identification sweeps
* Temporary relocations
* Work registration requirements
* Controlled distribution systems

Population stability is considered a strategic requirement.

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC BORDER INFRASTRUCTURE

## §6.1 Defensive Construction

Border infrastructure should prioritize:

* Obsidian reinforcement
* Blast-resistant construction
* Elevated sightlines
* Redundant communications
* Hardened supply depots
* Protected rail sectors
* Underground fallback access

Temporary structures are discouraged within strategic sectors.

---

## §6.2 Hidden Installations

Coalition doctrine authorizes concealed infrastructure within border regions including:

* Observation bunkers
* Emergency supply caches
* Tunnel access points
* Relay stations
* Strategic fallback rooms
* Concealed weapons storage
* Continuity shelters

Knowledge of hidden installations shall remain compartmentalized.

---

# SECTION VII — MISSILE RANGE PROXIMITY

## §7.1 Southwest Borderlands

The Southwest Borderlands shall remain under heightened strategic oversight due to presence of:

* Missile testing infrastructure
* Strategic launch sectors
* Restricted blast zones
* Deep underground facilities
* Experimental weapons systems

Civilian activity near designated testing regions is strongly discouraged.

---

## §7.2 Operational Concealment

Coalition personnel are prohibited from publicly discussing:

* Launch schedules
* Underground layouts
* Payload classifications
* Strategic deployment capability
* Testing frequency
* Restricted border operations

Operational ambiguity is considered strategically beneficial.

---

# SECTION VIII — STRATEGIC JUSTIFICATION

The Coalition recognizes borderlands not as empty territory, but as active defensive systems.

The border is not merely where territory ends.

It is where continuity begins defending itself.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A peaceful border is not achieved through trust.
It is achieved through the constant possibility of response.
