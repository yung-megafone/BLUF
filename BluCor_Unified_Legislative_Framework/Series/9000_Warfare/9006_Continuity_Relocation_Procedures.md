# PURPOSE

This directive establishes procedures for relocation of personnel, civilians, records, strategic materials, command staff, detainees, and infrastructure functions during emergencies threatening Coalition continuity.

Relocation shall be treated as a controlled preservation measure, not evacuation by panic.

---

# SECTION I — RELOCATION CATEGORIES

Relocation categories shall include:

## Category A — Command Continuity
High Table personnel, WXBRO personnel, strategic command staff, and authorized continuity officers.

## Category B — Security Continuity
BWARS, NSIC, tribunal staff, detention staff, and infrastructure security personnel.

## Category C — Technical Continuity
Engineers, power operators, redstone specialists, medical staff, archive staff, treasury staff, and communications workers.

## Category D — Civilian Stabilization
Registered residents relocated to prevent panic, exposure, starvation, or hostile capture.

## Category E — Restricted Persons
Detainees, tribunal subjects, sanctioned persons, and witnesses requiring controlled movement.

---

# SECTION II — PRIORITY ORDER

During major continuity events, relocation priority shall be:

1. Command continuity personnel  
2. Strategic records and authentication materials  
3. Communications personnel  
4. Treasury and reserve personnel  
5. Power and infrastructure operators  
6. Security forces  
7. Medical personnel  
8. Registered civilians  
9. Nonessential property  

Property preservation shall never outrank continuity personnel.

---

# SECTION III — RELOCATION SITES

Approved relocation sites may include:

- Underground shelters
- Hardened command sectors
- Borderland fallback posts
- Residential deep levels
- Rail-linked bunkers
- Tyrants Refuge auxiliary sectors
- ReCor emergency housing
- Classified continuity sites

Exact site locations may be restricted.

---

# SECTION IV — MOVEMENT CONTROL

Relocation shall occur through controlled corridors using:

- Registered groups
- Staged movement windows
- BWARS oversight
- Identity verification
- Restricted baggage rules
- Silence protocols where required
- Route compartmentalization

Unauthorized divergence from relocation routes may be treated as evasion.

---

# SECTION V — RECORD PRESERVATION

Priority records include:

- Constitutional documents
- Treasury ledgers
- Classified directives
- Personnel registries
- Facility maps
- Strategic authentication keys
- Tribunal records
- NSIC continuity archives

Records may be relocated before civilians where continuity requires.

---

# SECTION VI — CIVILIAN MANAGEMENT

Civilian messaging shall emphasize:

- Temporary safety movement
- Infrastructure protection
- Resource distribution
- Public order
- Compliance requirements

Details regarding threat severity may be withheld.

---

# SECTION VII — STRATEGIC JUSTIFICATION

A government that cannot move cannot survive.

Continuity depends on the ability to relocate authority, records, and essential personnel faster than collapse can consume them.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

Retreat is disorder.  
Relocation is continuity.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
