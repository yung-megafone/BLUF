# DIRECTIVE 9003

## STRATEGIC MISSILE DEPLOYMENT STANDARDS

### Warfare Doctrine Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L2`  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR / BWARS`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This directive establishes Coalition standards for the classification, placement, concealment, maintenance, and authorized strategic use of missile systems within BSC-controlled territory.

Missile systems shall be treated as strategic deterrence infrastructure and may not be deployed, relocated, armed, inspected, or decommissioned without proper command authorization.

---

# SECTION I — MISSILE CLASSIFICATIONS

## §1.1 Tactical Systems

Tactical missile systems are authorized for battlefield defense, border response, and localized infrastructure protection.

## §1.2 Strategic Systems

Strategic missile systems are reserved for high-value deterrence, retaliatory capability, and continuity preservation.

## §1.3 Concealed Systems

Concealed systems may be placed within:

- Borderlands
- Industrial zones
- Underground silos
- Abandoned infrastructure
- Reinforced bunkers
- Civilian-disguised service facilities

Concealed status shall not be disclosed to standard personnel.

---

# SECTION II — DEPLOYMENT STANDARDS

Missile sites shall prioritize:

- Structural concealment
- Blast containment
- Redundant command links
- Restricted access
- Surveillance coverage
- Independent power
- Emergency lockdown capability

Sites must not be located where accidental detonation would compromise primary continuity command unless authorized by [[1105_WXBRO_Strategic_Oversight|WXBRO]] review.

---

# SECTION III — SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

All missile sites shall maintain:

- Controlled access
- Personnel logs
- Tamper monitoring
- Dual authorization procedures
- Internal and external surveillance
- Physical hardening
- Emergency denial protocols

Unauthorized entry into a missile site constitutes a Security Offense and may escalate to [[2103_High_Treason_Statutes|High Treason]].

---

# SECTION IV — AUTHORIZATION

Missile launch authorization requires:

- Valid command order
- Confirmed target classification
- Operational necessity review
- Communications authentication
- Post-launch record sealing

Strategic launches require elevated command authorization.

Emergency launch doctrine shall remain separately classified.

---

# SECTION V — CONCEALMENT

Missile sites may be disguised as:

- Warehouses
- Utility buildings
- Service bunkers
- Monitoring stations
- Abandoned structures
- Survey facilities
- Borderland infrastructure

Civilian discovery shall trigger immediate containment review.

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC JUSTIFICATION

A missile system need not be fired to serve its purpose.

A known missile deters.
A hidden missile preserves uncertainty.
A distributed missile network ensures no single defeat removes Coalition capability.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

Deterrence is strongest when the enemy cannot count what remains.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
