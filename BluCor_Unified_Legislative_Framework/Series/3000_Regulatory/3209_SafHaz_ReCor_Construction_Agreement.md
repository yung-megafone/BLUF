# DIRECTIVE 3209

## SAFHAZ / RECOR CONSTRUCTION AGREEMENT

### Freedom Dr. Redevelopment Contract

CLASSIFICATION: `L1 / INTERNAL`
STATUS: `ACTIVE`
AUTHORITY: `SAFEHAZ / RECOR / TREASURY AUTHORITY`
AUTHORIZED BY: `INFRASTRUCTURE REVIEW BOARD`

---

# PURPOSE

This agreement establishes the formal construction and redevelopment arrangement between SafHaz and [[1105_ReCor_Infrastructure_Charter|ReCor]] regarding the approved reconstruction and development of Lots 50 and 60 Freedom Dr.

The agreement authorizes ReCor to conduct demolition, remediation, engineering, and construction operations associated with the proposed administrative and office complex approved under [[3208_Freedom_Dr_Acquisition_Authorization|Directive 3208]].

---

# DEVELOPMENT AUTHORIZATION

ReCor is hereby authorized to:

* Conduct structural demolition operations,
* Stabilize remaining infrastructure,
* Remove hazardous debris,
* Perform underground integrity surveys,
* Restore utility infrastructure,
* Construct reinforced administrative facilities,
* Integrate security and continuity systems,
* And preserve designated historical architectural elements.

All redevelopment operations shall remain subject to Treasury and SafHaz oversight throughout all construction phases.

---

# COST STRUCTURE

The parties agree that all material pricing associated with the project shall be calculated using State infrastructure material rates rather than commercial market pricing.

Approved State logistical reserves, industrial stockpiles, and Coalition-controlled production channels may therefore be utilized where available.

Commercial inflationary pricing models shall not apply unless expressly authorized by Treasury review.

---

# PROFIT LIMITATION

ReCor agrees that total realized profit margins associated with the redevelopment project shall not exceed:

`5% OF TOTAL VERIFIED PROJECT COST`

This limitation includes:

* subcontractor aggregation,
* material markup,
* labor markup,
* logistical overhead,
* and indirect administrative recovery fees.

The Treasury Authority reserves the right to conduct financial audits throughout all operational phases to ensure compliance.

Artificial inflation of project costs, concealed subcontract structures, or undisclosed procurement manipulation shall constitute Economic Violations under Coalition Penal Doctrine.

---

# HISTORICAL PRESERVATION REQUIREMENTS

The parties acknowledge the historical significance associated with the Freedom Dr district and former Building 3A operational sectors.

ReCor therefore agrees that the completed structure shall preserve architectural characteristics reflective of:

* pre-war Coalition defensive architecture,
* the original Building 3A structural identity,
* or the surviving deteriorated appearance presently associated with the district.

Approved preservation considerations may include:

* reinforced industrial facades,
* exposed armored structural elements,
* memorial walls,
* preserved foundation remnants,
* defensive-era architectural motifs,
* and integrated historical displays.

The redevelopment shall avoid excessive modernization inconsistent with the historical character of the district.

---

# SECURITY PROVISIONS

Given the facility's proximity to SafHaz RadiSafe operations, the completed structure shall include:

* reinforced access control systems,
* controlled perimeter routing,
* integrated surveillance infrastructure,
* hardened utility corridors,
* restricted maintenance access points,
* and emergency continuity support capability.

All underground excavation activity shall be reported to SafHaz oversight personnel prior to execution.

---

# LIABILITY

ReCor assumes operational responsibility for:

* construction-site safety,
* structural compliance,
* environmental containment,
* worker supervision,
* and authorized demolition procedures.

SafHaz assumes responsibility for:

* post-construction operational integration,
* internal administrative deployment,
* and long-term facility management.

---

# FINAL PROVISION

The parties jointly recognize that the redevelopment of the Freedom Dr district represents not merely a construction project, but the preservation and continuation of historically significant Coalition ground.

The structures raised upon these lots shall therefore serve both operational necessity and historical remembrance.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
