# PURPOSE

This directive establishes mandatory containment, storage, transportation, monitoring, and disposal standards for hazardous, radioactive, chemical, biological, and strategic industrial materials within Coalition territory.

---

# SECTION I — DESIGNATED FACILITY

All hazardous and strategic materials shall be stored, processed, transferred, or disposed through:

## SafHaz RadiSafe Facility
30 Freedom Dr.

Unauthorized independent storage is prohibited.

---

# SECTION II — CONTROLLED MATERIALS

Controlled materials include:

- Radioactive compounds
- Reactor byproducts
- Yellorium
- Chemical toxins
- Biological agents
- Strategic fuels
- Experimental industrial compounds
- Orbital materials
- Restricted research compounds

---

# SECTION III — CONTAINMENT STANDARDS

Facilities handling hazardous materials shall maintain:

- Reinforced containment sectors
- Ventilation redundancy
- Emergency isolation systems
- Surveillance overlap
- Spill control systems
- Underground shielding
- Access logging
- Environmental monitoring

---

# SECTION IV — TRANSPORTATION

Transport of hazardous materials requires:

- Approved routing
- BWARS notification
- Sealed containment
- Transfer manifests
- Escort authorization where required

Unauthorized movement constitutes Strategic Hazard Violations.

---

# SECTION V — INCIDENT RESPONSE

Containment breaches may authorize:

- District lockdown
- Evacuation
- Tunnel sealing
- Controlled demolition
- Emergency quarantine
- NSIC intervention

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Hazardous materials are continuity risks before they become industrial assets.

Containment failure threatens both infrastructure and legitimacy.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A material becomes strategic the moment its release threatens the population.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
