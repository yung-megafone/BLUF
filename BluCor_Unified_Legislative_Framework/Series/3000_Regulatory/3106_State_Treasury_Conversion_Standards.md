# ARTICLE X — CONVERSION RATES

## § 10-1 Primary Valuation Standard

Base Treasury valuation shall be denominated in Dollars (`$`).

### Approved Base Rates

|Material|Treasury Value|
|---|---|
|Diamond|`$6400`|
|Emerald|`$6400`|
|Gold Ingot|`$500`|
|Iron Ingot|`$100`|
|Nether Star|`$25600`|

---

## § 10-2 Rate Adjustment Authority

The Treasury reserves authority to adjust valuation rates in response to:

- scarcity,
    
- resource instability,
    
- wartime disruption,
    
- inflation,
    
- or legislative amendment.
    

All rate modifications shall be archived for continuity reference.

---

# ARTICLE XI — TRANSACTION TAXATION

## § 11-1 Material Transactions

No direct transaction fees shall apply to:

- material exchange,
    
- Treasury conversion,
    
- or State-approved reserve valuation operations.
    

---

## § 11-2 Currency Transactions

All currency-based transactions are subject to Treasury taxation.

### Historical Treasury Rates

|Revision Era|Tax Rate|
|---|---|
|2024 Standard|`25%`|
|Modern Treasury Amendment|`28%`|

The applicable Treasury tax shall be absorbed into the declared transaction value.

---

> [!warning] TREASURY TAX NOTICE  
> Unauthorized avoidance of Treasury transaction taxation constitutes Economic Fraud under Coalition Penal Doctrine.

---

# ARTICLE XII — RESERVE BANK REGULATIONS

## § 12-1 Reserve Bank Restrictions

Reserve Banks are prohibited from:

- charging unauthorized transaction fees,
    
- manipulating Treasury valuations,
    
- or profiting from unofficial taxation systems.
    

---

## § 12-2 State Protections

State-recognized Reserve Banks are afforded the same legal protections as Coalition financial infrastructure and BluCor assets.

Attacks against Reserve Banks may constitute:

- sabotage,
    
- economic terrorism,
    
- or High Treason depending upon severity.
    

---

# ARTICLE XIII — CONTINUITY OF ECONOMIC SYSTEMS

The Coalition recognizes standardized valuation systems as essential to economic continuity and State operational stability.

The Treasury therefore maintains permanent authority over:

- reserve valuation,
    
- exchange standards,
    
- and approved financial conversion systems.
