# DIRECTIVE 3204

## CIVIC SECURITY INFRASTRUCTURE MODERNIZATION

### Regulatory Infrastructure Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L0`  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BCIA / NSIC`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This directive establishes updated continuity and public safety infrastructure standards for all Coalition municipalities, transportation sectors, and designated continuity districts.

The initiative authorizes expanded deployment of:

- public surveillance systems,
    
- emergency acoustic monitoring,
    
- continuity telemetry infrastructure,
    
- and municipal coordination networks.
    

The purpose of these systems is to improve:

- emergency response,
    
- infrastructure stabilization,
    
- continuity resilience,
    
- and public safety coordination during periods of civil disruption or environmental instability.
    

---

# SECTION I — AUTHORIZED SYSTEMS

Authorized systems include:

- Municipal roadway cameras
    
- Transit monitoring systems
    
- Public emergency microphones
    
- Infrastructure telemetry arrays
    
- Continuity communication repeaters
    
- Environmental hazard monitoring systems
    
- Automated emergency alert infrastructure
    

Municipalities may request additional support through [[1112_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] continuity channels where elevated infrastructure concerns exist.

---

# SECTION II — PUBLIC SAFETY ASSURANCES

The Coalition affirms:

- No invasive personal tracking systems are authorized under this directive
    
- No residential monitoring may occur without existing judicial authorization
    
- Infrastructure systems shall remain restricted to public continuity and safety functions
    
- Civilian movement shall not be actively restricted under standard operational conditions
    

All authorized systems shall remain subject to continuity oversight requirements.

---

# SECTION III — CONTINUITY OPERATIONS

During periods of:

- civil instability,
    
- infrastructure compromise,
    
- strategic emergency,
    
- or continuity failure,
    

temporary monitoring expansion measures may be authorized to support:

- evacuation,
    
- emergency routing,
    
- continuity restoration,
    
- and public order stabilization.
    

Such measures remain temporary unless otherwise extended under Continuity Preservation Statutes.

---

# SECTION IV — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Modern continuity infrastructure requires visibility across critical public systems.

Public safety cannot exist where infrastructure blindness prevents coordination during instability events.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

The most effective continuity systems are those which integrate into ordinary civilian life with minimal disruption.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
