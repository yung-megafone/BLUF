# PURPOSE

This directive establishes infrastructure, security, operational, and continuity standards for Coalition transportation systems including rail, roadway, underground transit, and strategic logistics corridors.

---

# SECTION I — STRATEGIC TRANSPORT STATUS

Transportation infrastructure is recognized as critical continuity infrastructure.

Protected systems include:

- Rail lines
- Underground transit
- Strategic roads
- Freight corridors
- Maintenance tunnels
- Emergency relocation routes

---

# SECTION II — SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

Transportation infrastructure shall maintain:

- Surveillance systems
- Checkpoint integration
- Emergency shutdown capability
- Redundant routing
- Structural inspection schedules
- BWARS access capability

---

# SECTION III — RESTRICTED INTERFERENCE

The following are prohibited:

- Rail sabotage
- Unauthorized track modification
- Checkpoint bypass
- Freight tampering
- Tunnel obstruction
- Infrastructure mapping without authorization

---

# SECTION IV — EMERGENCY PRIORITY

During emergencies, transportation priority shall be assigned to:

1. Continuity personnel  
2. Strategic materials  
3. Security forces  
4. Medical operations  
5. Treasury transfers  
6. Civilian relocation  

---

# SECTION V — UNDERGROUND RAIL SYSTEMS

Underground transit systems shall maintain:

- Blast doors
- Emergency lighting
- Surveillance overlap
- Ventilation redundancy
- Segment isolation capability

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Transportation determines how quickly continuity can move.

A disconnected state becomes multiple vulnerable states.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

Infrastructure does not merely move people.  
It moves authority.
