# PURPOSE

This directive establishes surveillance integration standards for residential infrastructure throughout Coalition territory.

The Coalition recognizes surveillance as essential to maintaining public order, rapid emergency response, and continuity stability.

---

# SECTION I — REQUIRED SYSTEMS

Approved residential developments shall maintain:

- Hallway surveillance
- Entrance monitoring
- Elevator observation
- Access logging
- Emergency alert integration
- Underground corridor monitoring

---

# SECTION II — DATA RETENTION

Residential surveillance records shall be retained for durations determined by NSIC and BWARS review.

Records may be archived indefinitely where strategic review requires.

---

# SECTION III — RESTRICTED MODIFICATIONS

Residents may not:

- Disable surveillance systems
- Obstruct cameras
- Tamper with wiring
- Create blind spots
- Construct unauthorized hidden rooms

---

# SECTION IV — ACCESS AUTHORITY

Authorized personnel may review surveillance data for:

- Criminal investigations
- Infrastructure review
- Population monitoring
- Continuity emergencies
- Hazard response
- Counterintelligence operations

---

# SECTION V — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Surveillance reduces uncertainty.

Reduced uncertainty increases continuity stability.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Residents are reminded:

A monitored building is easier to protect than an unknown building.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
