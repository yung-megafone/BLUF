# DIRECTIVE 8107

## SAFHAZ PROPERTY ACQUISITION REQUEST

### Freedom Dr Expansion Proposal

CLASSIFICATION: `L1 / INTERNAL`  
STATUS: `UNDER REVIEW`  
AUTHORITY: `SAFEHAZ / TREASURY AUTHORITY / RECOR`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `PROPERTY REVIEW BOARD`

---

# OVERVIEW

SafHaz formally submits a request for the acquisition of Lots 50 and 60 Freedom Dr for the proposed sum of `300,000` payable to the State Treasury.

The requesting party argues that the lots in question have remained abandoned for multiple years and that the associated structures have deteriorated beyond reasonable recovery. Existing buildings upon the properties have partially collapsed and continue to degrade structurally.

SafHaz further argues that the condition of these abandoned facilities presents an unnecessary operational and security concern due to their immediate proximity to one of the mandatory access routes servicing the SafHaz RadiSafe Secure Storage Facility located at 30 Freedom Dr.

---

# PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT

Pending approval, SafHaz proposes the demolition, remediation, stabilization, and redevelopment of the affected lots into a secured administrative and office complex intended to support:

- Regulatory operations
    
- Hazardous material oversight
    
- Administrative coordination
    
- Long-term infrastructure management
    
- Emergency continuity support operations
    
- ReCor and Treasury liaison functions
    

The proposed facility would include reinforced utility infrastructure, secured access routing, controlled surveillance integration, and expanded logistical staging capacity.

---

# SECURITY JUSTIFICATION

SafHaz asserts that the continued abandonment of the adjacent lots creates multiple avoidable security vulnerabilities including:

- Concealed unauthorized access routes
    
- Structural collapse hazards
    
- Reduced visibility around secured transport corridors
    
- Potential misuse by unauthorized persons
    
- Obstruction of emergency response access
    
- Increased long-term maintenance liability
    

Given the proximity of the deteriorating structures to a protected strategic facility, SafHaz recommends that redevelopment be considered a matter of infrastructure security rather than conventional commercial acquisition alone.

---

# HISTORICAL PRESERVATION STATEMENT

SafHaz additionally requests that the review board recognize the historical significance of the proposed acquisition site.

The deteriorating grounds surrounding Building 3A represent one of the most historically significant surviving locations associated with the NOVI-War.

SafHaz reminds the board that these lots once marked the final operational territory maintained by BluCor before the tide of the conflict reversed.

The structures that now deteriorate beneath weather and neglect are the remnants of facilities that endured months of siege conditions, sustained bombardment, repeated assaults, structural fires, and prolonged defensive operations.

Despite the destruction surrounding the valley, Building 3A remained operational far longer than the encampments and forward positions it once supervised.

The requesting party argues that the land itself has surpassed the battlefield it once defended and now stands not merely as abandoned property, but as a surviving historical scar within Coalition territory.

SafHaz therefore proposes that portions of the redevelopment preserve select remnants or memorial elements associated with the historical site.

---

`300,000`
