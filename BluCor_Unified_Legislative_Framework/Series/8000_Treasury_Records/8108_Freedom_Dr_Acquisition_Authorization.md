# DIRECTIVE 8108

## FREEDOM DR ACQUISITION AUTHORIZATION

### Historical Preservation and Redevelopment Order

CLASSIFICATION: `L1 / INTERNAL`  
STATUS: `APPROVED`  
AUTHORITY: `TREASURY AUTHORITY / RECOR / SAFEHAZ`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `PROPERTY REVIEW BOARD`

---

# AUTHORIZATION

Following formal review of acquisition request `3207_SafHaz_Property_Acquisition_Request`, the Treasury Authority hereby authorizes the transfer and acquisition of Lots 50 and 60 Freedom Dr by SafHaz for the approved amount of:

`300,000`

The acquisition is approved under the determination that redevelopment of the deteriorating properties serves both strategic infrastructure and public safety interests.

---

# DEVELOPMENT CONDITIONS

Approval of redevelopment is contingent upon the following requirements:

- Structural demolition and remediation shall be conducted under ReCor oversight.
    
- Historically significant remnants deemed structurally recoverable shall be preserved where feasible.
    
- The completed administrative complex shall maintain architectural continuity with either:
    
    - the surviving pre-war appearance of Building 3A, or
        
    - the existing architectural profile currently present upon the lots.
        

The review board formally requests that the replacement structures honor the visual identity and historical character associated with the NOVI-War defensive period.

---

# HISTORICAL DESIGNATION

The Treasury Authority recognizes Building 3A and the surrounding grounds as historically significant sites within Coalition territory.

The board acknowledges that the facilities occupying these lots once represented critical defensive infrastructure during the NOVI-War and that the surviving structures continue to hold symbolic and historical value to the Coalition.

The requested architectural continuity is therefore not merely aesthetic in nature, but commemorative.

The reconstruction effort shall aim to preserve:

- defensive-era visual characteristics,
    
- reinforced industrial architectural motifs,
    
- structural proportions associated with the original installations,
    
- and memorial elements acknowledging the site's wartime significance.
    

---

# PRESERVATION STATEMENT

The review board recognizes that the structures now deteriorating along Freedom Dr once stood during one of the most unstable periods in Coalition history.

The surviving facilities endured repeated bombardment, prolonged siege conditions, internal collapse events, structural fires, and sustained assault operations during the NOVI-War.

Despite the destruction surrounding the valley and the repeated seizure attempts against Coalition territory, portions of the original facilities remained standing.

The board therefore concludes that the preservation of the architectural identity associated with Building 3A represents a matter of historical continuity rather than simple infrastructure preference.

---

# FINAL AUTHORIZATION

SafHaz may proceed with:

- property transfer,
    
- demolition review,
    
- environmental remediation,
    
- engineering surveys,
    
- and phased redevelopment planning.
    

Construction operations shall remain subject to ReCor and Treasury compliance inspection throughout all phases of development.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
