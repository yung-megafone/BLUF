# DIRECTIVE 8110

## FREEDOM DR BILL OF SALE

### Transfer of Lots 50 and 60 Freedom Dr

CLASSIFICATION: `L1 / INTERNAL`  
STATUS: `EXECUTED`  
AUTHORITY: `TREASURY AUTHORITY / SAFEHAZ / RECOR`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `PROPERTY REVIEW BOARD`

---


# OVERVIEW

This document serves as the formal bill of sale and authorized transfer agreement for Lots 50 and 60 Freedom Dr from the State Treasury to SafHaz under the authority granted by the Coalition Property Review Board.

The properties are transferred for the purposes of redevelopment, historical preservation, strategic infrastructure stabilization, and administrative expansion operations associated with the SafHaz RadiSafe containment district.

---

# PROPERTY DESIGNATION

Transferred Properties:

- Lot 50 Freedom Dr
    
- Lot 60 Freedom Dr
    

Previous Ownership:

`STATE TREASURY`

New Ownership:

`SAFEHAZ`

---

# SALE AMOUNT

Base Property Acquisition Amount:

`300,000`

Applicable State Infrastructure Transaction Tax:

`28%`

Calculated Tax Amount:

`84,000`

---

# TOTAL TRANSFER COST

Total Amount Due to State Treasury:

`384,000`

---

# PAYMENT CONDITIONS

SafHaz agrees to remit the total amount in full to the Treasury Authority under the following conditions:

- Funds shall be transferred through authorized Treasury channels only.
    
- Transaction records shall remain archived under Coalition infrastructure acquisition records.
    
- Payment shall be completed prior to commencement of major construction operations.
    
- Treasury Authority reserves audit authority over all associated redevelopment expenditures.
    

---

# DEVELOPMENT CONDITIONS

Transfer approval remains contingent upon:

- compliance with Directive 3208,
    
- preservation review requirements,
    
- ReCor engineering approval,
    
- and continued adherence to historical continuity guidelines.
    

The purchasing party additionally agrees that redevelopment efforts shall maintain architectural respect toward the historical defensive-era structures previously occupying the lots.

---

# HISTORICAL DESIGNATION NOTICE

The Treasury Authority formally recognizes the transferred district as historically significant Coalition ground associated with the NOVI-War defensive campaigns.

Ownership transfer does not remove the historical designation associated with the properties.

SafHaz agrees to preserve select architectural, memorial, or historical elements where operationally feasible.

---

# LIABILITY TRANSFER

Upon execution of payment and transfer authorization:

- SafHaz assumes responsibility for property maintenance,
    
- environmental remediation,
    
- structural stabilization,
    
- and redevelopment compliance.
    

The Treasury Authority relinquishes direct maintenance responsibility following final transfer execution.

---

# FINAL PROVISION

The Coalition recognizes this transfer not merely as a financial transaction, but as the continuation and preservation of historically significant territory central to the survival and continuity of the BSC during one of the most unstable periods in Coalition history.
