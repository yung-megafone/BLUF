# Section 1: Overview

Article XXV establishes the operational framework permitting select:

```text
OrCor personnel
```

to operate within Coalition national security strategy under compartmentalized authority.

Authorized personnel may conduct:

- classified operations,
    
- strategic countermeasures,
    
- continuity enforcement,
    
- and autonomous security initiatives
    

while maintaining operational separation from BluCor public identity structures.

---

# Section 2: Security Clearance and Autonomy

---

# Subsection 2.1 — Authorization for Clearance

## Clearance Status

Select OrCor personnel are granted:

```text
Level 5 Clearance
```

with indefinite integration into the:

```text
BluCor Strategization Complex (BSC)
```

security framework.

---

## Operational Autonomy

Authorized personnel shall operate independently of public BluCor administration to preserve:

- deniability,
    
- compartmentalization,
    
- and continuity secrecy.
    

Personnel remain subject to prosecution under:

- [[2075_High_Severity_Crimes]]
    
- and continuity enforcement doctrine  
    if accountability failures arise.
    

---

# Subsection 2.2 — Facility Authority

## Authorized Facilities

Approved personnel may establish:

- anonymous black sites,
    
- continuity shelters,
    
- compartmentalized operational facilities,
    
- and:
    

```text
Sensitive Compartmented Information Facilities (SCIFs)
```

for operations deemed critical to national security.

---

## Operational Secrecy

Such facilities must:

- appear autonomous,
    
- maintain separation from BluCor public infrastructure,
    
- and conceal continuity affiliations  
    to preserve strategic deniability.
    

---

# Subsection 2.3 — Authorization of Force

## Use of Force Authority

OrCor personnel possessing Level 5 Clearance are authorized to employ:

```text
any force necessary
```

to preserve:

- operational secrecy,
    
- facility integrity,
    
- strategic assets,
    
- and continuity doctrine.
    

---

## Security Enforcement

Individuals found:

- trespassing,
    
- interfering,
    
- surveilling,
    
- or compromising protected operations
    

shall face:

- immediate detainment,
    
- continuity interrogation review,
    
- and strategic enforcement action.
    

---

# Section 3: National Security Operations and Oversight

---

# Subsection 3.1 — AOP Program Integration

Authorized OrCor personnel may participate in:

```text
All Other Projects (AOP)
```

programs involving:

- strategic continuity operations,
    
- compartmentalized initiatives,
    
- and restricted national security projects.
    

Oversight shall remain under the:

```text
BluCor Intelligence Committee
```

---

# Subsection 3.2 — Reporting and Immunity

## National Security Protection

Operations conducted under Article XXV remain:

- exempt from public review,
    
- shielded from civilian oversight,
    
- and protected under continuity security doctrine.
    

---

## Immunity Clause

Operational immunity shall remain in effect unless:

```text
unanimous consent
```

from the BWARS Oversight Panel determines an operation:

- egregious,
    
- unnecessary,
    
- or strategically destabilizing.
    

---

# Section 4: Special Clauses

---

# Subsection 4.1 — NSD-GPD Award Clause

Recipients of the:

```text
NSD-GPD Award
```

shall retain conditional immunity from legal accountability under BULF in recognition of exceptional service to Coalition continuity operations.

---

## Immunity Revocation

Such immunity becomes void where:

- malicious intent,
    
- deliberate sabotage,
    
- or hostile conduct  
    against BluCor or affiliated entities is established.
    

---

# Subsection 4.2 — National Security Protection Clause

Authorized OrCor personnel may:

- detain,
    
- isolate,
    
- and process
    

individuals violating continuity or national security protocols upon protected facilities.

---

## Enforcement Procedures

Violations may invoke procedures pursuant to:

- [[2181_Enhanced_Interrogation_Guidelines]]
    
- counter-espionage doctrine
    
- continuity detention standards
    
- and strategic security enforcement.
