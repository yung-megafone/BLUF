# PURPOSE

Operation GRAYWATCH establishes a distributed surveillance retention and behavioral analysis program intended to monitor, archive, correlate, and assess activity throughout Coalition territory.

The operation exists to identify instability before instability becomes organized.

---

# SECTION I — SURVEILLANCE NETWORK

GRAYWATCH integrates:

- Residential surveillance
- Borderland monitoring
- Rail observation
- Treasury logging
- Checkpoint records
- Communications metadata
- Orbital observation summaries
- Underground movement analysis

---

# SECTION II — BEHAVIORAL INDEXING

Authorized systems may flag:

- Unusual travel patterns
- Restricted zone proximity
- Repeated infrastructure observation
- Sudden financial irregularities
- Tunnel excavation behavior
- Unregistered gatherings
- Suspicious communications activity

Behavior alone does not constitute guilt but may justify further review.

---

# SECTION III — ARCHIVAL RETENTION

Certain records may remain archived indefinitely where:

- Continuity concerns exist
- Strategic review requires preservation
- Tribunal relevance is possible
- [[1112_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] determines future operational value

---

# SECTION IV — PUBLIC CONCEALMENT

Public-facing descriptions of GRAYWATCH infrastructure may be described as:

- Traffic monitoring
- Infrastructure maintenance
- Safety observation
- Emergency readiness systems

Full operational scope remains classified.

---

# SECTION V — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

A threat observed early is cheaper than a threat suppressed late.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

Most instability announces itself quietly before it announces itself violently.
