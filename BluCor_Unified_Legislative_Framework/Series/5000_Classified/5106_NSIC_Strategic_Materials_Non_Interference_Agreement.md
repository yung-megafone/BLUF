# DIRECTIVE 5106

## NSIC STRATEGIC MATERIALS NON INTERFERENCE AGREEMENT

### Classified Operations Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L4`  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR / NSIC`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This directive establishes strategic coexistence procedures governing [[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]]-controlled radioactive and nuclear-capable materials.

---

# SECTION I — NON-INTERFERENCE CONDITIONS

The Coalition agrees not to interfere with designated [[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] material operations provided:

- Materials remain within authorized territory
- Fallout does not exceed containment thresholds
- Unauthorized proliferation does not occur
- Strategic reporting remains compliant

---

# SECTION II — MATERIAL SHARING

[[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] shall provide the Coalition with:

- Energy surplus
- Reactor byproducts
- Strategic waste materials
- Research-adjacent industrial outputs

Transferred materials become Coalition property.

---

# SECTION III — TERRITORIAL LIMITS

[[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] activity remains restricted to approved containment sectors.

Unauthorized expansion voids protections.

---

# SECTION IV — PUBLIC CONCEALMENT

Public doctrine shall continue to state:

- Nuclear activity remains restricted
- Strategic materials are heavily controlled
- Coalition enforcement remains uncompromised

Existence of this agreement remains classified.

---

# SECTION V — CONTINGENCY AUTHORITY

Should containment fail, the Coalition reserves authority to enact:

- Seizure
- Lockdown
- Infrastructure denial
- Strategic intervention
- Continuity emergency declaration

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Controlled risk is preferable to uncontrolled collapse.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A tolerated threat remains tolerated only while it remains predictable.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
