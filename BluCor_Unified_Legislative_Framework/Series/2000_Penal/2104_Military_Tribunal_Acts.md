# DIRECTIVE 2104

## MILITARY TRIBUNAL ACTS

### Penal and Judicial Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L1`  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR / BWARS`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This directive establishes the authority, jurisdiction, procedures, and emergency powers of Coalition military tribunals.

---

# SECTION I — JURISDICTION

Military tribunals may review cases involving:

- [[2103_High_Treason_Statutes|High Treason]]
- Classified operations
- Strategic sabotage
- Continuity threats
- Restricted infrastructure
- Borderland insurgency
- Missile systems
- Orbital deterrence
- Hazard containment breaches

---

# SECTION II — CLASSIFIED PROCEEDINGS

Tribunals may operate:

- Privately
- Under seal
- Without public disclosure
- Under compartmentalized authority

Evidence may remain classified indefinitely.

---

# SECTION III — EVIDENTIARY AUTHORITY

Tribunals may consider:

- Surveillance records
- [[1112_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] reports
- Treasury audits
- Classified testimony
- Strategic assessments
- Communications intercepts

Standard public evidentiary rules may be suspended.

---

# SECTION IV — EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

During Continuity Emergencies, tribunals may:

- Accelerate sentencing
- Restrict appeals
- Expand detention authority
- Operate continuously
- Conduct sealed review

---

# SECTION V — SENTENCING AUTHORITY

Tribunals may impose:

- Detainment
- Forced labor
- Asset seizure
- Administrative reassignment
- District exclusion
- Execution where authorized

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Military tribunals exist to preserve continuity when ordinary judicial pace becomes operationally dangerous.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A state at risk does not always have the luxury of slow judgment.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
