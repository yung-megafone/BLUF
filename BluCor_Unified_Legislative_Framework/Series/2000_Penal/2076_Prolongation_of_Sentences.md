# § 76-1 Definition of Especially Heinous Acts

## Criteria

Acts deemed especially heinous include but are not limited to:

### A.

Mass atrocities involving:

- catastrophic loss of life,
    
- widespread destruction,
    
- or severe civilian suffering.
    

### B.

Acts of betrayal resulting in:

- continuity destabilization,
    
- strategic collapse,
    
- or catastrophic operational consequences for BluCor or affiliated entities.
    

### C.

Crimes exhibiting:

- egregious cruelty,
    
- deliberate psychological torment,
    
- prolonged suffering,
    
- or intentional infliction of severe harm.
    

---

# § 76-2 Sentence Prolongation Protocol

## Mandate

Individuals convicted of especially heinous crimes may be subjected to procedures intended to artificially prolong lifespan and preserve continued incarceration capacity.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of sentence prolongation is to ensure:

- extended accountability,
    
- sustained punishment,
    
- continuity preservation,
    
- and prevention of natural expiration prior to sentence fulfillment.
    

---

# § 76-3 Methods of Prolongation

## Techniques

Approved methods may include:

### A. Biological Enhancement

Use of:

- medical intervention,
    
- regenerative treatment,
    
- biochemical enhancement,
    
- or forced vitality preservation
    

to maintain physical survivability.

---

### B. Nutritional Sustenance

Administration of life-preserving sustenance intended to:

- prevent natural expiration,
    
- maintain bodily function,
    
- and prolong incarceration duration.
    

---

### C. Physical Maintenance

Periodic medical procedures intended to:

- prevent organ failure,
    
- reduce natural degeneration,
    
- and preserve long-term prisoner survivability.
    

---

### D. Monitoring

Continuous monitoring and evaluation of prisoner condition to ensure:

- maximum longevity,
    
- sustained sentence viability,
    
- and preservation of punitive intent.
    

---

# § 76-4 Conditions of Prolonged Incarceration

## Environment

Prisoners subjected to sentence prolongation shall be housed within specialized isolation facilities designed to:

- minimize sensory stimulation,
    
- restrict environmental variation,
    
- and preserve containment integrity.
    

---

## Restrictions

Prisoners subject to this protocol shall be denied:

- unrestricted interaction,
    
- environmental variation,
    
- natural sunlight exposure,
    
- or unsanctioned communication.
    

Communication with:

- staff,
    
- detainees,
    
- or external entities
    

is prohibited except where explicitly authorized.

---

# § 76-5 Authorization and Oversight

## Authority

Authorization for sentence prolongation remains exclusively reserved for:

- the High Court of BWARS,
    
- and designated continuity judicial authorities.
    

Cases require approval by no fewer than:

```text
three senior judges
```

prior to implementation.

---

## Documentation

Records regarding:

- conviction,
    
- sentence status,
    
- medical procedures,
    
- and continued detention review
    

shall remain classified and maintained by the:

```text
BluCor Intelligence and Compliance Division
```

---

# § 76-6 Termination of Prolonged Sentences

## Conditions for Termination

Sentence prolongation protocols may terminate upon determination that the prisoner has reached:

- maximum physical degradation,
    
- irreversible psychological collapse,
    
- or continuation failure conditions.
    

---

## Execution Authority

Execution procedures associated with continuity detention doctrine may be implemented at the discretion of the overseeing authority pursuant to:

- judicial authorization,
    
- continuity preservation standards,
    
- and strategic detention review.
