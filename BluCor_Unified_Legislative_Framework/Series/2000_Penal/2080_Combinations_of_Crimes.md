# § 80-1 Definition of Crime Combinations

## Criteria

A combination of crimes refers to:

- multiple offenses,
    
- simultaneous violations,
    
- or sequential criminal conduct
    

which collectively amplify:

- severity,
    
- continuity risk,
    
- operational harm,
    
- or strategic destabilization.
    

---

## Examples

Examples include but are not limited to:

### A.

Substance-related offenses pursuant to:

- [[2069_Substance_Related_Crimes]]
    

combined with:

- property crimes,
    
- sabotage,
    
- or infrastructure offenses  
    under:
    
- [[2070_Serious_Crimes]]
    

---

### B.

Acts of treason pursuant to:

- [[2075_High_Severity_Crimes]]
    

committed alongside:

- violence against BluCor personnel,
    
- attacks on continuity infrastructure,
    
- or organized insurgent activity.
    

---

# § 80-2 Enhanced Sentencing for Crime Combinations

## Mandate

Combinations of crimes shall incur enhanced judicial penalties including but not limited to:

### A.

Immediate classification as:

```text
Especially Heinous Acts
```

under:

- [[2076_Prolongation_of_Sentences]]
    

---

### B.

Automatic escalation toward:

- capital punishment,
    
- indefinite incarceration (`INDEF`),
    
- continuity detention,
    
- or sentence prolongation protocols
    

depending upon:

- severity,
    
- continuity impact,
    
- operational damage,
    
- and public destabilization.
    

---

# § 80-3 Compound Sentences

## Concurrent Offenses

Where multiple offenses occur simultaneously:

- sentencing shall be compounded,
    
- and penalties applied consecutively  
    to ensure full accountability.
    

---

## Example

A violator convicted of:

- property theft,
    
- sabotage,
    
- or Treasury violations
    

during a declared continuity emergency may face cumulative prosecution under all applicable statutes.

---

## Sequential Offenses

Crimes committed over time with demonstrated intent to:

- evade justice,
    
- destabilize continuity,
    
- or inflict cumulative harm
    

may trigger additional penalties pursuant to:

- [[2075_High_Severity_Crimes]]
    
- continuity escalation doctrine
    
- and strategic judicial authority.
    

---

# § 80-4 Linking Articles for Maximum Enforcement

## Cross-Referencing

Crimes involving:

- intoxicants,
    
- sabotage,
    
- violence,
    
- organized resistance,
    
- or attacks upon state personnel
    

may trigger immediate implementation of:

- enhanced sentencing,
    
- continuity escalation,
    
- sentence prolongation,
    
- or classified detention procedures.
    

---

## Escalation Examples

### A.

Substance-related offenses targeting BluCor personnel or continuity assets may invoke:

- [[2076_Prolongation_of_Sentences]]
    
- and strategic detention authority.
    

---

### B.

Destruction of property during organized resistance operations may escalate charges to include:

- rebellion,
    
- conspiracy,
    
- or High Treason.
    

---

### C.

Especially heinous acts causing:

- public harm,
    
- environmental devastation,
    
- or continuity destabilization
    

may qualify for:

- compounded prosecution,
    
- enhanced sentencing,
    
- and indefinite detention review.
    

---

# § 80-5 Determination of Combined Crimes

## Authority

The:

```text
High Court of BWARS
```

shall retain sole authority to:

- adjudicate compound criminal cases,
    
- determine linked charges,
    
- and assign enhanced sentencing.
    

---

## Oversight

Oversight of:

- sentence execution,
    
- continuity detention,
    
- sentence prolongation,
    
- and indefinite incarceration
    

shall remain under the authority of:

- Tyrant’s Refuge MS administration,
    
- continuity judicial officials,
    
- and Intelligence and Compliance personnel.
    

---

# § 80-6 Special Cases for Repeat Offenders

## Escalated Measures

Repeat offenders demonstrating persistent patterns of combined criminal conduct may be designated as:

```text
Persistent Threat Entities (PTEs)
```

---

## Enforcement

PTE designation may result in:

- enhanced surveillance,
    
- indefinite detention review,
    
- sentence prolongation consideration,
    
- or strategic containment procedures.
    

---

## Post-Incarceration Violations

Further violations following incarceration may result in:

- summary execution review,
    
- permanent reassignment to experimental prolongation programs,
    
- or continuity-level detention protocols.
    

---

# § 80-7 Documentation and Oversight

## Mandatory Records

All compound criminal cases shall be:

- thoroughly documented,
    
- cross-referenced,
    
- archived,
    
- and indexed
    

with references to all applicable articles and sentencing authorities.

---

## Oversight Authority

The:

```text
BluCor Intelligence and Compliance Division
```

shall maintain:

- detailed judicial archives,
    
- enforcement consistency reviews,
    
- and continuity sentencing records.
