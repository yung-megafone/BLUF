# CHARTER 1102

## NSIC OPERATIONAL AUTHORITY

### Governmental Authority Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L1`  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This charter establishes the National Strategic Intelligence Command ([[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]]) as the Coalition’s classified intelligence, counterintelligence, surveillance, and continuity preservation authority.

---

# SECTION I — CORE RESPONSIBILITIES

[[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] responsibilities include:

- Counterintelligence
- Surveillance integration
- Classified investigations
- Threat assessment
- Strategic monitoring
- Information control
- Continuity intelligence
- Orbital observation coordination

---

# SECTION II — CLASSIFIED AUTHORITY

[[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] operations may remain:

- Undisclosed
- Compartmentalized
- Sealed
- Denied publicly
- Exempt from ordinary reporting

Operational secrecy is considered essential to continuity.

---

# SECTION III — SURVEILLANCE AUTHORITY

[[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] may conduct:

- Communications monitoring
- Facility observation
- Strategic auditing
- Population analysis
- Threat indexing
- Informant cultivation

Certain surveillance operations may proceed without public awareness.

---

# SECTION IV — CONTINUITY PRIORITY

[[1102_NSIC_Operational_Authority|NSIC]] shall prioritize:

1. Continuity preservation  
2. Strategic secrecy  
3. Infrastructure survival  
4. Counter-sabotage  
5. Internal threat suppression  

---

# SECTION V — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Information is infrastructure.

A state that loses control of information loses control of continuity itself.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

The strongest surveillance system is the one the population stops noticing.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
