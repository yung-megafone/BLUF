# Article I: Founding and Purpose

## 1 - Establishment of RedCor

RedCor is designated as the Top-Secret Experimental Research Division operating under the direct oversight of BluCor.

---

## 2 - Purpose and Function

RedCor existed to conduct highly classified research and development (`R&D`) projects, including:

- Advanced and experimental technological innovation.
    
- Testing and analysis of sensitive or unstable technologies.
    
- Development of technologies deemed critical to the state’s strategic security.
    
- Strategic experimentation involving compartmentalized systems and continuity-sensitive technologies.
    

---

## 3 - Chain of Command

RedCor operated as a restricted-access entity fully subordinate to BluCor, with all findings immediately submitted to BluCor for:

- documentation,
    
- analysis,
    
- continuity review,
    
- and strategic application.
    

---

# Article II: Authority & Operational Restrictions

## 1 - Operational Freedom

RedCor was granted restricted autonomous authority to:

- Design, test, and operate classified research facilities.
    
- Develop projects deemed sensitive or of significant strategic interest.
    
- Maintain secrecy surrounding the location and operation of testing grounds and research initiatives.
    
- Conduct compartmentalized experimentation under SCI handling protocols.
    

---

## 2 - Restricted Personnel Requirements

All RedCor personnel were required to:

- Operate under strict two-person teams for all projects to minimize information exposure.
    
- Remain permanently within RedCor-designated facilities during active projects.
    
- Abstain from external communications of any kind while possessing or working with `SCI` (`Sensitive Compartmented Information`).
    
- Submit to continuity-era monitoring and internal security review.
    

---

## 3 - Information Control and Reporting

RedCor personnel were required to immediately submit all:

- findings,
    
- progress reports,
    
- discoveries,
    
- technological developments,
    
- and experimental outcomes
    

to BluCor leadership.

Failure to do so constituted treasonous activity.

---

# Article III: High Secrecy Mandates

## 1 - Classified Restrictions

The following restrictions were imposed upon all RedCor operations:

- No external personnel were permitted to access RedCor facilities.
    
- RedCor findings could not be disseminated to any individuals outside the immediate project team or BluCor leadership.
    
- RedCor personnel were forbidden from leaving designated facilities until project completion or reassignment.
    
- Unauthorized archival indexing was prohibited.
    

---

## 2 - Egregious Treason Clause

Any breach of RedCor secrecy, including:

- unauthorized communication,
    
- dissemination,
    
- external disclosure,
    
- or failure to submit findings
    

resulted in:

- Immediate forfeiture of all personnel rights.
    
- Classification of the violation as `Egregious Treason`.
    
- Immediate state response authority.
    
- Indefinite detainment or disciplinary action as determined by BluCor leadership.
    

---

# Article IV: Dissolution of RedCor

## 1 - Deemed Treasonous

RedCor was formally deemed treasonous following:

- instigation of nonexistent operational threats,
    
- repeated insubordination,
    
- failure to meet strategic deadlines,
    
- internal destabilization,
    
- and violations of continuity oversight doctrine.
    

---

## 2 - Sentencing and Excommunication

All RedCor personnel lacking active knowledge of SCI systems or continuity-sensitive operations were ordered to:

- Be excommunicated to predetermined RedStone City sectors for a minimum mandatory term of `30 days`.
    
- Forfeit all property, facilities, technologies, and associated infrastructure.
    
- Submit all archival material for immediate seizure by the State Complex.
    

---

## 3 - Conditional Reinstatement

Following the 30-day sentence period, the Board retained authority to hold a reinstatement vote concerning RedCor operations.

Even in the event of unanimous approval, BluCor retained sole authority to deny re-entry or reactivation of RedCor infrastructure in the interest of:

- state preservation,
    
- continuity integrity,
    
- and strategic stability.
