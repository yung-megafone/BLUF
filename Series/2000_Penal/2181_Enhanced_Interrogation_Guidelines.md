# § 81-1 Purpose and Scope

Enhanced interrogation techniques are authorized exclusively for extraction of:

- critical intelligence,
    
- operational information,
    
- continuity threats,
    
- or strategic data
    

directly impacting:

- BluCor operations,
    
- Coalition security,
    
- continuity preservation,
    
- or the welfare of affiliated personnel and entities.
    

---

# § 81-2 Authorization

## Approval Process

Enhanced interrogation procedures may only be authorized by:

### A.

A BluCor High Command official.

### B.

The:

```text
Director of Compliance and Enforcement (DCE)
```

---

## Exception Authority

In cases involving immediate operational threat,  
field officers possessing rank equivalent to:

```text
Colonel or higher
```

may authorize temporary measures pending formal review.

---

## Documentation Requirement

All enhanced interrogation actions must:

- be documented,
    
- recorded,
    
- and submitted for judicial review
    

within:

```text
72 hours of initiation
```

by the High Court.

---

# § 81-3 Techniques Permitted

## Authorized Measures

The following techniques are authorized within continuity-approved operational parameters.

---

## A. Physical Conditioning

Authorized procedures include:

- extended standing,
    
- stress positioning,
    
- environmental manipulation,
    
- and controlled physical exhaustion.
    

Non-lethal pain induction measures,  
including nerve stimulation devices,  
may be utilized where authorized.

---

## B. Sensory Manipulation

Approved methods include:

- sensory deprivation,
    
- controlled sensory overload,
    
- light manipulation,
    
- sound manipulation,
    
- and tactile disorientation procedures.
    

---

## C. Psychological Tactics

Authorized psychological measures include:

- isolation,
    
- disorientation,
    
- mock execution procedures,
    
- psychological profiling,
    
- and exploitation of identified fears or vulnerabilities.
    

---

## D. Chemical Methods

Approved non-lethal chemical methods may include substances intended to:

- impair memory,
    
- reduce resistance,
    
- induce suggestibility,
    
- or promote truthfulness.
    

All chemical compounds remain subject to:

- continuity classification controls,
    
- restricted inventory management,
    
- and compliance oversight.
    

---

# § 81-4 Restrictions and Prohibited Acts

## Prohibited Actions

The following actions are expressly prohibited unless superseded by continuity emergency authority:

### A.

Infliction of permanent physical mutilation or irreversible bodily damage.

### B.

Interrogation procedures resulting in death without explicit authorization under strategic judicial authority.

### C.

Use of techniques not formally outlined under approved doctrine without High Court authorization.

---

# § 81-5 Oversight and Compliance

## Monitoring

All interrogation sessions involving enhanced procedures must:

- be monitored in real-time,
    
- continuously recorded,
    
- and archived for continuity review.
    

---

## Audits

The:

```text
BluCor Intelligence Oversight Committee (BIOC)
```

shall conduct quarterly audits to:

- ensure procedural compliance,
    
- review operational effectiveness,
    
- and identify unauthorized conduct.
    

---

## Penalties for Misuse

Personnel engaging in:

- unauthorized methods,
    
- excessive force,
    
- prohibited procedures,
    
- or continuity violations
    

may be subject to:

- disciplinary review,
    
- treason investigation,
    
- Persistent Threat Entity (`PTE`) designation,
    
- or indefinite detention.
    

---

# § 81-6 Special Cases

## High-Value Targets

Enhanced interrogation authority may be escalated for:

- individuals charged with treason,
    
- continuity threats,
    
- insurgent coordinators,
    
- or designated high-value targets.
    

---

## Prolonged Sentence Subjects

Subjects undergoing:

- indefinite incarceration,
    
- sentence prolongation,
    
- or continuity detention protocols
    

may be utilized for:

- training,
    
- procedural testing,
    
- or operational evaluation
    

with prior High Command authorization.

---

# § 81-7 Ethical Standards

## Humane Treatment Clause

Authorized personnel shall ensure interrogation procedures do not produce:

- unjustifiable suffering,
    
- uncontrolled physical damage,
    
- or operational excess
    

beyond the intended objective of intelligence extraction.

---

## Mandatory Training

All personnel authorized to conduct enhanced interrogation procedures must complete:

```text
Enhanced Interrogation Certification (EIC)
```

on an annual basis.
