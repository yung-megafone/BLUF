# DIRECTIVE 2101

## CRIMINAL OFFENSES

### Penal and Judicial Series

CLASSIFICATION: `L0  
STATUS: `ACTIVE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR / BWARS`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# PURPOSE

This directive establishes the primary criminal code governing conduct within Coalition territory.

All persons within Coalition jurisdiction remain subject to this code regardless of citizenship status.

---

# SECTION I — GENERAL OFFENSES

Criminal offenses include but are not limited to:

- Theft
- Assault
- Unauthorized entry
- Vandalism
- Trespassing
- Property destruction
- Illegal excavation
- Smuggling
- Forgery
- Fraud
- Unauthorized surveillance
- Checkpoint evasion
- Curfew violation

---

# SECTION II — SECURITY OFFENSES

Security Offenses include:

- Restricted zone intrusion
- Unauthorized photography
- Surveillance interference
- Infrastructure sabotage
- Strategic information disclosure
- Tunnel construction near protected sites
- Illegal weapons possession
- Impersonation of Coalition personnel

---

# SECTION III — PUBLIC ORDER OFFENSES

Public Order Offenses include:

- Riot participation
- Organized unrest
- Incitement
- Panic dissemination
- Unauthorized assembly
- Distribution of destabilizing propaganda
- Obstruction of emergency personnel

---

# SECTION IV — CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES

## §69 — Drug Offenses

The following are prohibited without explicit Coalition authorization:

- Cultivation of hemp products
- Possession of hemp byproducts
- Cocaine manufacture
- Cocaine distribution
- Narcotics trafficking
- Chemical synthesis operations
- Unlicensed pharmaceuticals
- Biological intoxicants

Possession, cultivation, transport, sale, or concealment of controlled substances may result in:

- Asset seizure
- Forced labor sentencing
- Detainment
- Tribunal escalation

Strategic narcotics production may constitute Economic Sabotage.

---

# SECTION V — SENTENCING

Sentencing may include:

- Fines
- Labor reassignment
- Curfew restrictions
- Asset forfeiture
- Detainment
- District exclusion
- Administrative relocation
- Tribunal escalation

Repeat offenders may face enhanced penalties.

---

# SECTION VI — STRATEGIC DOCTRINE

Crime is not merely unlawful conduct.

Crime destabilizes continuity by introducing unpredictability into controlled systems.

---

# OPERATIONAL NOTE

Personnel are reminded:

A stable civilization criminalizes instability first.

---

END OF DIRECTIVE
