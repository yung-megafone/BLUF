# PUBLIC NOTICE 7122

## HISTORICAL STATEMENT ON BUILDING 1A

### A Testament to Coalition Endurance

CLASSIFICATION: `L0 / PUBLIC RELEASE`  
STATUS: `PUBLIC RELEASE`  
AUTHORITY: `BLUCOR / PUBLIC AFFAIRS BUREAU`  
AUTHORIZED BY: `HIGH TABLE`

---

# OVERVIEW

The Coalition formally recognizes Building 1A as one of the most historically significant surviving structures within the lands of the BSC.

Constructed during the earliest expansion period of Coalition territory, Building 1A has persisted through territorial collapse, prolonged warfare, bombardment campaigns, occupation attempts, infrastructure failure, sabotage operations, and some of the harshest periods recorded within Coalition history.

Today, the structure remains standing as both a physical landmark and a symbol of endurance.

---

# THE NOVI-WAR

During the NOVI-War, the surrounding valley and defensive sectors became the site of prolonged and devastating conflict.

Historical records indicate that Coalition forces endured:

- repeated artillery bombardment,
    
- siege conditions lasting multiple months,
    
- sustained assaults against strategic facilities,
    
- widespread infrastructure collapse,
    
- and repeated territorial incursions by hostile forces.
    

Building 1A changed hands multiple times during the conflict and suffered extensive structural damage throughout the war period.

Despite these events, the structure continued to operate in partial capacity even during some of the conflict's most unstable periods.

---

# THE WITHERSPOON INCIDENT

Coalition records further document the March 2020 infiltration and temporary overrunning of Building 1A during one of the conflict's most unstable operational periods.

Historical accounts state that Captain Witherspoon successfully navigated the sealed and partially compromised interior of the facility following hostile occupation.

Despite communication failures and severe internal damage, Captain Witherspoon reportedly established contact with defensive personnel operating from Building 3A and successfully transmitted a distress signal requesting reinforcement support.

By the time Coalition reinforcements reached the facility, reports indicate that Captain Witherspoon had already neutralized the hostile traitor elements occupying the structure.

The incident remains one of the most referenced examples of defensive persistence associated with the NOVI-War period.

---

# MISSILE OPERATIONS

In June 2020, the primary entrance sector of Building 1A was converted into a strategic missile deployment platform as emergency wartime measures escalated throughout the valley.

Historical launch records indicate that hundreds of payloads were deployed from the converted launch tubes during the height of the conflict.

In July 2020, resistance forces successfully sabotaged the launch infrastructure in an attack that nearly leveled major portions of the facility.

Coalition engineers later concluded that the advanced defensive armoring integrated into the structure was likely the primary reason catastrophic structural collapse did not occur.

Significant portions of the reinforced defensive shell remain intact to this day.

---

# THE SOUTHWEST SUBTERRANEAN DISASTER

Building 1A additionally survived one of the most catastrophic industrial incidents recorded within Coalition territory.

Historical summaries describe a chain of failures occurring within the southwest subterranean laboratory sectors beneath the facility, ultimately resulting in the inadvertent detonation of a powerful nuclear payload.

The resulting blast completely destroyed:

- the laboratory sector,
    
- multiple adjacent containment chambers,
    
- and thousands of cubic meters of underground material.
    

Internal reports indicate that the blast wave reached as far as the final defensive doors of the secure containment wing.

Despite the severity of the event, Building 1A itself remained structurally intact and radiation containment measures successfully prevented external contamination of the surrounding territory.

The incident continues to be studied as one of the most significant examples of successful containment infrastructure within Coalition history.


---

# SYMBOLISM

Today, Building 1A stands not merely as surviving infrastructure, but as a reminder of the resilience required to preserve stability during periods of extreme uncertainty.

Its walls, once scarred by bombardment and conflict, now represent the strength required to overcome instability and preserve the future of the Coalition.

The Coalition recognizes the building not simply as a structure, but as a historical monument to endurance, sacrifice, continuity, and survival.

---

# PUBLIC ACCESS NOTICE

Due to the continued operational sensitivity and strategic importance associated with portions of the structure, internal mechanisms, layouts, infrastructure schematics, and historical containment sectors associated with Building 1A remain restricted from public disclosure.

Imagery and structural documentation relating to secured internal sectors are rarely released to the public.

---

# PRESERVATION INITIATIVE

BluCor and ReCor jointly confirm that future redevelopment efforts surrounding the district shall attempt to preserve architectural continuity and historical elements associated with the original defensive-era structures wherever feasible.

The Coalition further encourages continued archival preservation efforts relating to the NOVI-War period to ensure that future generations understand the sacrifices associated with the survival of the lands.
