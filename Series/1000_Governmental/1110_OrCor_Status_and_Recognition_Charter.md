# PURPOSE

This charter formally establishes the Coalition-recognized political status of the organization commonly designated as:

`OrCor`

within Coalition archival, governmental, and BWARS operational systems.

This document additionally clarifies the relationship between OrCor, the BluCor Strategization Complex, and the territories surrounding RedStone City.

---

# SECTION I — STATUS OF ORCOR

OrCor is not recognized as:

- a subordinate branch of the BluCor Strategization Complex,
    
- an official Coalition authority,
    
- nor a governing component of the BSC administrative structure.
    

Rather, OrCor is publicly classified as an independent extremist-origin coalition operating primarily from the RedStone City region and adjacent territorial sectors.

---

# SECTION II — PUBLIC DESIGNATION

Within public Coalition doctrine and BWARS classification systems, OrCor is generally identified as:

- an extremist political coalition,
    
- a destabilizing territorial entity,
    
- and a historically hostile force originating from the RedStone City theater.
    

Historical BWARS records associate OrCor-aligned elements with:

- sabotage operations,
    
- territorial incursions,
    
- infrastructure attacks,
    
- continuity disruption attempts,
    
- and wartime hostility during the NOVI-War period.
    

---

# SECTION III — TERRITORIAL ORIGINS

Coalition historical records indicate that OrCor influence emerged primarily from sectors surrounding:

`RedStone City`

during periods of regional instability preceding and during the NOVI-War.

The Coalition recognizes RedStone City as one of the most politically volatile operational regions in recorded Coalition history.

---

# SECTION IV — CURRENT RELATIONS

While OrCor remains publicly classified as an external extremist-origin coalition, Coalition leadership acknowledges that:

- territorial coexistence,
    
- strategic noninterference,
    
- and continuity preservation measures
    

have at times necessitated indirect communication, containment agreements, or restricted diplomatic interaction.

Specific operational arrangements, if any exist, may remain subject to classification restrictions.

# SECTION V — BWARS AUTHORITY

BWARS retains authority to:

- monitor OrCor activity,
    
- restrict hostile operations,
    
- defend Coalition infrastructure,
    
- and respond to strategic threats associated with hostile territorial expansion or continuity interference.
    

Nothing within this charter shall be interpreted as:

- formal political endorsement,
    
- territorial surrender,
    
- or Coalition subordination to OrCor authority.
    

---

# SECTION VI — HISTORICAL INTERPRETATION

Coalition historians continue to debate:

- the origins of OrCor,
    
- the political fragmentation preceding the NOVI-War,
    
- and the degree to which historical territorial collapse contributed to modern Coalition instability.
    

Public historical interpretations may therefore differ substantially from restricted archival records.

---

# FINAL PROVISION

The Coalition recognizes that not all threats originate externally, and not all hostile entities emerge without historical cause.

The existence of OrCor remains both a security concern and a permanent reminder of the instability that once nearly fractured the lands entirely.
