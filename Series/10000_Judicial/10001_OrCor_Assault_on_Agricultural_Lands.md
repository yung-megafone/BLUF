# THE HIGH COURT OF BWARS

## CASE NUMBER: HC-20241224-0945

### ORCOR ASSAULT ON AGRICULTURAL LANDS

CLASSIFICATION: `L0 / PUBLIC`  
STATUS: `CLOSED`  
AUTHORITY: `HIGH COURT OF BWARS`

---

> [!info] PUBLIC JUDICIAL RECORD  
> This document represents a publicly archived High Court proceeding concerning the destruction of protected agricultural infrastructure operating under BWARS jurisdiction.

---

# Date

`2024-12-24`

---

# Judges Presiding

- Justice Elara Sovrin (`Chief Justice`)
    
- Justice Mathias Korven
    
- Justice Lina Tharok
    

---

# Overview

## Incident Summary

On or about `2024-12-24` at approximately `09:45`, OrCor, an entity originating within BlaCor to secure the lands of RedStone City, engaged in an assault upon and caused damage to agricultural lands located at coordinates:

```text
-1909, 332
```

The damaged property consisted of a small farming fort situated over two kilometers from BroCor’s primary operating lands but associated with agricultural stewardship obligations outlined under:

- Article IV-1 of the [[1107_BroCor_Agricultural_Charter]].
    

---

# Arguments Presented

# Prosecution (High Court)

## I.

The farming fort, despite its distance from BroCor’s primary operational zone, falls under BroCor protection and responsibility pursuant to Article IV-1 of the charter.

---

## II.

The hostile faction residing in the nearby village located at:

```text
-1905, 522
```

was not present at the site, and no evidence of hostile activity was observed at the time of the incident.

---

## III.

BroCor obligations to ensure ethical and sustainable agricultural practices extend to all factions operating under BWARS jurisdiction, including villages not directly governed by BroCor.

---

# Defense (OrCor Team Leader Greg Gewlardi)

## I.

The distance between the incident location and BroCor recognized operational zones created ambiguity concerning the land’s protected status.

---

## II.

The farmland’s association with a hostile village justified the action, as OrCor personnel faced ongoing hostility from the faction operating in that region.

---

## III.

OrCor acted within its mandate to secure RedStone City lands and possessed no reasonable means of identifying the fort’s agricultural purpose at the time of engagement.

---

## IV.

Articles III-1 and III-2(a) of the [[1107_BroCor_Agricultural_Charter]] permitted search and entry operations involving force during hostile-land operations intended to secure facilities.

OrCor asserted their actions fell within these provisions.

---

## V.

OrCor personnel operated under the [[1106_BlaCor_Defense_Development_Charter]] and therefore claimed qualified immunity.

---

# Counterarguments (BluCor Officials)

## I.

BlaCor personnel do not possess equivalent authority to BluCor under the BroCor Agricultural Charter.

Qualified immunity as claimed by OrCor does not extend to BlaCor personnel under BWARS legal doctrine.

---

## II.

The provisions referenced under Articles III-1 and III-2(a) did not apply within this instance, as the property was:

- not actively hostile,
    
- not under confirmed threat,
    
- nor designated as an active national security concern
    

at the time of the assault.

---

# Deliberation

The High Court reviewed:

## I.

The [[1107_BroCor_Agricultural_Charter]], particularly:

- Articles IV-1,
    
- IV-2,
    
- III-1,
    
- and III-2(a),
    

which outline BroCor stewardship authority over agricultural resources and investigatory doctrine.

---

## II.

OrCor’s operational mandate to secure RedStone City lands.

---

## III.

Testimonies and evidentiary submissions provided by both parties.

---

The Court determined that:

- the fort in question served an agricultural purpose,
    
- no hostile personnel were present,
    
- and no immediate threat existed at the time of the assault.
    

The Court further ruled that:

- BroCor protections explicitly extend to agricultural operations under BWARS jurisdiction regardless of direct governance status,
    
- and the defense interpretation of Articles III-1 and III-2(a) lacked legal applicability in the absence of formal national security authorization.
    

The Court additionally rejected OrCor immunity claims, ruling that:

- BlaCor personnel authority does not equate to BluCor sovereign authority under BWARS law.
    

---

# Verdict

```text
GUILTY
```

---

# Charges

## I.

Violation of BroCor agricultural protections pursuant to Article IV-1 of the charter.

---

## II.

Unauthorized destruction of protected agricultural assets.

---

# Sentencing

## I.

Greg Gewlardi, Team Leader of OrCor, is hereby sentenced to:

- life imprisonment,
    
- with eligibility for parole review after twenty-five (`25`) years.
    

---

## II.

OrCor shall compensate BroCor for all damages incurred.

Reparations shall total:

```text
75 metric tons of raw materials
```

equivalent in value to the destroyed agricultural resources.

---

## III.

OrCor personnel involved in the assault shall undergo mandatory BWARS protocol training focused upon:

- agricultural land protections,
    
- civilian infrastructure preservation,
    
- and charter compliance procedures.
    

---

## IV.

OrCor may continue operations within RedStone City but shall remain restricted to:

- defensive operations,
    
- and self-defense engagements only
    

until a formal governance charter is documented and approved.

---

## V.

A formal public apology from OrCor leadership to BroCor shall be published within seven (`7`) days affirming commitment to BWARS charter doctrine.

---

# Conclusion

This verdict reaffirms:

- the sanctity of agricultural operations under BroCor stewardship,
    
- and BWARS’ overarching obligation to protect continuity-era food infrastructure.
    

All entities operating under BWARS jurisdiction are reminded of their duty to distinguish between:

- hostile targets,
    
- civilian operations,
    
- and protected agricultural infrastructure
    

to prevent unnecessary conflict and preserve regional stability.
